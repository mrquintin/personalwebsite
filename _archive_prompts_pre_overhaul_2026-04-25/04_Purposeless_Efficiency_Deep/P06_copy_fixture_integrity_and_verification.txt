P06 — COPY, FIXTURE INTEGRITY, AND SHIP VERIFICATION
=====================================================

Prerequisites: P01, P02, P03, P04, P05 — each having delivered
their own acceptance clauses. This prompt does not add new
features. It does three things:

  1. Codifies the AUTHOR'S VOICE rules as a small, enforceable
     lint that runs at build time and at test time on every
     authored prose string in the PRP fixtures.
  2. Defines the FIXTURE INTEGRITY contract: the shape every
     fixture file must satisfy, the type-guard machinery that
     verifies it, and the unpublished-work IP check that
     prevents accidental leakage of manuscript prose.
  3. Specifies the SHIP VERIFICATION protocol: the full matrix
     of acceptance tests, a11y sweeps, performance budgets,
     and cross-mode state checks that must pass before the
     EXPERIENCE/DOSSIER toggle is flipped on for the PRP panel.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART A — AUTHOR VOICE LINT
----------------------------------------------------------------------

A1. The two modes of failure.

All authored prose in this suite must avoid two distinct
failure modes:

    MARKETING VOICE
        Hedged, hype-y, self-congratulatory, or ad-copy prose
        that a corporate marketing editor would approve. The
        book's voice does not permit it; the experience must
        not drift into it.

    ACADEMIC HEDGE
        Prose that qualifies itself to unreadability. "Arguably",
        "one might say", "some readers may feel that", "it
        could be argued that". The book's voice takes positions
        cleanly and either argues for them or declines to
        discuss them. The experience must not soften the book
        into a seminar hedge.

A2. The lint rules.

Implement as a node script at scripts/prp-prose-lint.ts that
reads every fixture file under src/lib/prp/*.ts, extracts every
string field marked as authored prose via the fixture type
(see Part B), and checks each against the following rules.
Failures exit the process with non-zero and print the offending
file, field, and excerpt.

Rule L01 (FORBIDDEN WORDS — marketing register).
    The following tokens are forbidden in any authored prose:
        "disruptive", "disruptive force", "paradigm shift",
        "game-changing", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary",
        "world-class", "next-generation", "best-in-class",
        "value proposition", "unlock", "unleash", "supercharge",
        "empower", "synergy", "leverage" (as verb),
        "seamless", "frictionless", "turnkey", "mission-
        critical", "at scale" (as sole qualifier), "robust"
        (alone, without a domain), "solution" (as a noun
        substitute for "approach" or "proposal"),
        "platform" (for anything that is not actually a
        platform), "journey" (for reading/learning), "story"
        (for argument), "narrative" (for argument),
        "thought leader", "take" (as noun, e.g., "a hot take").
    Check case-insensitively; exempt occurrences in
    double-quoted blocks representing objections or
    counter-positions (these strings are intentionally voiced
    in another register). See A4 for the exemption mechanism.

Rule L02 (FORBIDDEN PHRASES — academic hedge).
    Forbid, case-insensitively (again with A4 exemption):
        "it could be argued that",
        "one might say",
        "some readers may feel",
        "arguably",
        "some would say",
        "in a sense",
        "in some sense",
        "to some extent",
        "as it were",
        "per se" (when followed by a comma, used as filler),
        "if you will",
        "at the end of the day",
        "when all is said and done".

Rule L03 (ADJECTIVE INFLATION).
    No single string may contain more than 4 adjectives in
    any 120-character window. An adjective is tagged via a
    small wordlist (not by a full POS tagger). Wordlist:
    seed with roughly 400 common English adjectives; admit
    false-negatives (we are trying to catch inflation, not
    linguistically police the author).

Rule L04 (EM-DASH OVERUSE).
    No string may contain more than 2 em-dashes ("—") in a
    single sentence, and no paragraph more than 4 em-dashes.
    The Guide's own prose uses em-dashes heavily and the book
    does too; this rule is not about style, it is about
    preserving legibility at the small reveal-panel widths
    the experience runs in. When the rule trips, revise to
    parenthetical or semicolon.

Rule L05 (UNDEFINED CAPITALS).
    Any all-caps token that is not an approved claim ID (from
    claims.ts), thinker ID, stance label, mode name, step
    label, or response-kind badge, AND that exceeds 4 letters,
    must be defined at first appearance in the string or
    omitted. This catches accidental acronym invention.

Rule L06 (FORBIDDEN CLAIMS).
    The following phrases must never appear in any authored
    string (no exemptions; even in objection-voice these are
    off-limits):
        "buy now", "buy the book", "preorder", "sign up",
        "subscribe", "mailing list", "join our", "contact us",
        "learn more" (use a direct link or a sentence that
        names what the reader will learn).

A3. Author-approved overrides.

When a string legitimately must use a forbidden word (rare but
possible, e.g., the dojo's objection continuation option that
reads "Say the book is playing with words" might need
"rhetorical" which is otherwise suspect), the string field in
the fixture may be annotated:

    export interface AuthoredProse {
      text: string;
      lintOverrides?: Array<{ rule: "L01" | "L02" | "L03" | "L04" | "L05"; reason: string }>;
      authorApproved: boolean;
    }

Every override must include a one-line reason. Authorship of
overrides is implicitly Michael; CI does not attempt to verify
human identity. An override without a reason is a lint failure.

A4. Objection-voice exemption.

Tile A of the Objections Dojo (P04) renders the objection text,
which intentionally voices the critic, not the book. Fixture
strings flagged with kind: "objection-voice" are exempt from
L01 and L02 rules. Flag is a fixture field, not a comment.

A5. Sentence-length soft cap.

No SINGLE SENTENCE may exceed 50 words. The Guide's prose often
exceeds this; the experience is rendered at smaller widths and
requires tighter pacing. A lint warning, not a hard failure, is
printed on any authored sentence over 50 words.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART B — FIXTURE INTEGRITY
----------------------------------------------------------------------

B1. src/lib/prp/typeGuards.ts

Defines runtime type guards for every fixture shape. Runs once
at module load in dev and in the test bundle; shipped unused in
production (tree-shaken).

    import type { Claim, ClaimEdge } from "./claims";
    import type { QuadrantCase } from "./quadrantCases";
    import type { DiamondCase } from "./diamondCases";
    import type { Objection, Response } from "./objections";
    import type { Thinker } from "./thinkers";
    import type { GlossaryTerm } from "./glossary";

    export function assertFixtures(): void {
      assertClaims();
      assertEdges();
      assertQuadrantCases();
      assertDiamondCases();
      assertObjections();
      assertThinkers();
      assertGlossary();
      assertCrossReferences();
    }

Each assertX function verifies:

    - All required fields present.
    - Enum fields (stance, kind, verdict, quadrant, response
      kind, etc.) match the declared union exactly — no stray
      values.
    - authorApproved=true for every v1-ship item AND the string
      fields are non-empty and non-placeholder.
    - No string field contains the "[GUIDE PARAPHRASE — PENDING
      AUTHOR REVIEW]" prefix (this prefix is the drafting stub;
      its presence in a ship build is a P06 failure).
    - Numeric fields are in range (urgency 1-5; coordinates
      0-100; delta values in {-2,-1,0,1,2}).

assertCrossReferences verifies:

    - Every claim ID cited anywhere (quadrantCase.claimRefs,
      diamondCase.tagClaimIds, objection.targetClaimIds,
      response.openInOntologyClaimId, thinker.connectedClaimIds,
      glossary term.connectedClaimIds) exists in the CLAIMS
      array.
    - Every diamond caseId cited in response.openInDiamondCaseId
      exists in DIAMOND_CASES.
    - Every thinker ID referenced in any claim's thinkerRefs
      (if added) exists in THINKERS.

B2. Unpublished-work IP check.

The book is unpublished. The suite must enforce:

    - No authored prose string in the fixtures may claim to be
      a direct book excerpt.
    - A specific lint (L07) runs over authored prose and fails
      if a string both (a) exceeds 200 characters and (b)
      matches a regex that suggests it is excerpted (starts
      with an opening quotation mark and ends with closing
      quotation, has an em-dash-attribution "— Purposeless
      Efficiency, Ch.X", etc.).
    - Fixture items that DO want to include a short excerpt
      must use a dedicated field shape:

          export interface BookExcerpt {
            text: string;                      // <= 200 chars
            chapter: number;
            permissionGranted: true;
            preReleaseApproval: "author-approved-for-web";
            approvedOn: string;                // ISO date
          }

      Fields that hold BookExcerpt are declared at the type
      level so that the lint cannot confuse excerpts with
      narration.

    - No fixture module may import a text file from a path
      outside src/lib/prp/. This blocks accidental inclusion
      of manuscript content via build tooling.

B3. Authoring workflow.

A fixture item may be in one of three states:

    DRAFT
        authorApproved=false. Draft text may contain the
        "[GUIDE PARAPHRASE — PENDING AUTHOR REVIEW]" prefix.
        The item will NOT be rendered in production builds; in
        the explorer mode it appears as a stub (e.g., "claim
        pending author review").

    AUTHOR-REVIEWED
        authorApproved=true. Text must pass the full prose lint.
        This is the ship-ready state.

    RETIRED
        Items can be retired by adding retired=true and
        retiredReason. They remain in the data for reference but
        are filtered out at render time. This supports the book's
        own iterative working style.

State transitions happen by editing the fixture file; there is
no admin UI. Michael edits, the tests run, CI fails or passes.

PART C — SHIP VERIFICATION PROTOCOL
----------------------------------------------------------------------

C1. Test matrix.

Acceptance tests live under
src/components/projects/purposeless-efficiency/Experience/__tests__/.
Use the existing test harness (Vitest per prior prompts).

Required suites:

  chassis.spec.tsx
      - Mounts PrpExperience at NARROW/STANDARD/WIDE widths.
      - Verifies ModeTabBar tab order and active mode.
      - Verifies EXPERIENCE/DOSSIER toggle preserves dossier.
      - Verifies sessionStorage persistence of "prp.side".

  ontology.spec.tsx
      - Renders spine-only and all-claims views.
      - Keyboard navigation traverses nodes in authored order.
      - ClaimCard opens on Enter; ESC returns focus.
      - DEPENDS ON / ENTAILS links update selectedClaimId.
      - Footer "authored/total" count matches data.

  quadrant.spec.tsx
      - Chips render for all v1 cases.
      - Drag-to-drop works via pointer events; keyboard-equiv
        works via pick-up/move/drop.
      - Interview toggles; interview questions advance.
      - PlacementCompare shows agreement indicator correctly
        for same / adjacent / opposite placements.
      - Trajectory arrow renders for Sears and iPhone.

  diamond.spec.tsx
      - All six v1 cases render in grid.
      - Stepper advances 1 -> 2 -> 3 -> 4; BACK preserves input.
      - Step 4 cannot advance with all three consequences empty.
      - Submit shows user walk and book walk side by side.
      - Diagnostic block correctly flags Step 1 and Step 2
        differences.
      - ACCEPT / DISSENT / REDO all update store state.

  objections.spec.tsx
      - All seven objections render with correct urgency.
      - Picking one opens DialecticTurn on Tile A.
      - Tiles advance A -> B -> C.
      - Response-kind badge renders correct kind.
      - For REFRAME objections, addendum about "not expect to
        convert" renders.
      - Continuation selection persists across a re-open.
      - Rank affordance appears only after 3+ objections read.

  atlas.spec.tsx
      - All v1 thinkers render at correct orbit coords.
      - Stance colors correct.
      - ThinkerDrawer shows all required sections.
      - CONNECTED CLAIMS links route to Ontology.
      - COUNTER-RECOMMENDATION renders only when non-null.
      - Glossary strip visible across all five modes (mount
        order does not matter).
      - Full glossary search filters correctly.

  cross-mode.spec.tsx
      - Selecting a claim in Ontology persists across mode
        switches.
      - "Open in Diamond" from Objections preselects the
        correct diamond case.
      - "Open in Ontology" from Atlas preselects the correct
        claim.
      - Dossier toggle from any mode preserves the
        currently-active mode state (e.g., a diamond walk
        in-progress survives the round-trip).

  a11y.spec.tsx
      - No heading-order violations.
      - All interactive elements have accessible names.
      - All tab panels have tablist/tab/tabpanel roles.
      - All drawers trap focus inside them when open and
        return focus on close.
      - aria-live region receives the expected announcements
        on mode change, claim select, step advance, objection
        open, thinker focus.
      - reducedMotion media query mock forces opacity-only
      - Color contrast is WCAG AA or better on every semantic
        token used (--ok, --warn, --err, --fg, --fg-dim, --bg).

  prose-lint.spec.ts
      - Imports all fixture files and runs the lint rules.
      - Fails on any L01/L02/L04/L05/L06/L07 violation.
      - Reports L03 as test warnings.
      - Reports sentence-length warnings (A5).

  bundle-budget.spec.ts (or CI assertion step)
      - Measures gzipped size of the PRP experience bundle
        (code for all five modes + fixtures) and asserts it
        is under 80KB.
      - The 80KB total is the binding constraint from I01.
      - Per-mode CODE targets (excluding fixture data):
          Chassis        <=  6KB
          Ontology       <= 10KB
          Quadrant       <= 10KB
          Diamond        <= 10KB
          Objections     <= 10KB
          Atlas+Glossary <= 12KB
        Code subtotal target: <= 58KB.
      - FIXTURE DATA budget (all files under src/lib/prp/*)
        counted ONCE, not per-mode. Targets:
          claims.ts          <= 5KB gzip
          quadrantCases.ts   <= 3KB gzip
          diamondCases.ts    <= 4KB gzip
          objections.ts      <= 3KB gzip
          thinkers.ts        <= 3KB gzip
          glossary.ts        <= 4KB gzip
        Data subtotal target: <= 22KB.
      - Sum of code + data: <= 80KB.
      - If code-splitting is applied per-mode, the data for each
        mode's fixtures may be loaded on-demand, which improves
        FIRST PAINT but does not change the total transferred
        size if the user visits all modes. The 80KB binding
        constraint is on the WORST CASE sum (user visits every
        mode in the session).
      - The per-mode CODE budgets above are internal
        consistency targets; a mode that exceeds its target but
        keeps the total under 80KB is acceptable, flagged only
        as a drift warning in CI output.

C2. Manual verification protocol (before toggling EXPERIENCE on
for production).

Even with the above test matrix green, a manual pass is
required:

  - Open PRP panel at screen widths 360, 640, 960, 1280, 1440.
    Confirm no horizontal scroll, no clipping, no font-size
    regression.
  - Rotate through all five modes. Confirm:
        - Tab bar shows the correct active state.
        - No flash of unstyled content.
        - No layout shift when the drawer opens.
        - The glossary strip persists.
  - Run through one diamond walk. Confirm the book's walk
    reveals correctly for the selected case.
  - Read each of the seven objection tiles. Confirm the
    response-kind badge matches the text.
  - Tab through the entire experience without a mouse. Confirm
    every interactive element is reachable and usable.
  - Enable reduced-motion in the OS. Confirm no translations,
    no scale, no parallax.
  - Enable screen reader. Confirm aria-live announcements are
    useful (not spammy, not silent).
  - Run axe-core via dev-tools. Zero violations required.
  - Validate in Safari, Chrome, Firefox; on iOS Safari at
    mobile viewport. Known issue to watch: drag-and-drop
    polyfill on iOS Safari — must fall back to tap-to-pick,
    tap-to-drop path.
  - Build the production bundle. Inspect with a bundle analyzer
    to confirm no accidental inclusion of test fixtures or
    manuscript files.
  - Confirm the EXPERIENCE/DOSSIER toggle correctly preserves
    all Prompts 07-09 dossier content.

PART E — POST-SHIP BEHAVIOR
----------------------------------------------------------------------

After ship, the following are NOT auto-updated:

  - Fixture prose. Author edits manually; tests re-run on PR.
  - Thinker list. New thinkers added manually to thinkers.ts.
  - Orbit coordinates. Do not run auto-layout; coordinates are
    authored.

The following ARE expected to drift over time and should be
checked quarterly:

  - Quadrant cases: some institutions' trajectories will update
    (e.g., an AI lab's Q-position may move). Each case's
    trajectory field should include the year; author reviews
    the list annually.
  - Objections: if a new common objection emerges from reader
    feedback and the book acknowledges it, add to the seven.
    The canonical count is not sacred; the Guide lists seven
    because seven are what it lists, not because seven is a
    ceiling.
  - Glossary: the Guide has ~40 terms. The book's working
    files likely have more (the 55 build essays suggest
    technical vocabulary will grow).

None of these updates require a new prompt; they are
authoring-level maintenance.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART F — A NOTE TO MICHAEL
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The three rules in tension across this suite are:

  (i)  Render the book faithfully. Do not substitute a softer
       or more accessible argument for the book's actual one.
  (ii) Respect the reader. Do not grade, score, convert, or
       preach.
  (iii) Ship something that works. Every mode must be legible
        at 360px and must pass a11y.

When these rules appear to conflict — e.g., "fidelity to the
book's REBUKE of Fukuyama" vs. "respect for a reader who has
never read Fukuyama" — the answer is the COUNTER-RECOMMENDATION
field, the "you can open this in the Ontology" path, and the
methodology admission strip. Do not resolve the tension by
softening the book. Resolve it by giving the dissenting reader
a disciplined path to dissent.

The experience's success is measured by one thing: whether a
careful reader leaves it with a sharper sense of the book's
specific claims and their specific arguments. Not by excitement;
not by scroll depth; not by time on page. Those metrics lie.
The right metric is: can the reader, on their way out, name the
claim they reject first?

If yes, the experience has done its job.
