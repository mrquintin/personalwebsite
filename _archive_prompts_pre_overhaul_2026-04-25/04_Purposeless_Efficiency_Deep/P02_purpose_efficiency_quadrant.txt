P02 — PURPOSE-EFFICIENCY QUADRANT DIAGNOSTIC (MODE 2 of 5)
===========================================================

Prerequisites: P01 (chassis + Ontology Explorer delivered).

GOAL OF THIS MODE

Teach the user the book's diagnostic lens — the Purpose-Efficiency
Framework — by having them use it on institutions they already
have intuitions about, then showing where the book places those
institutions and why. The Framework is not a mood board of
quadrant names; it is an analytical procedure with specific
questions, specific evidence classes, and specific failure modes.
This mode makes the procedure legible by letting the user walk it.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART A — WHAT THE FRAMEWORK IS, FOR THE IMPLEMENTER
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The Purpose-Efficiency Framework (PEF) is a 2x2 matrix with:

    X-axis  EFFICIENCY       LOW  <-----------------> HIGH
                             (form, speed, polish of execution)

    Y-axis  PURPOSE           LOW  <-----------------> HIGH
                             (coherence of the justifying argument
                              for the institution's existence)

Four quadrants:

    Q1  LOW purpose, LOW  efficiency     STRUGGLING / NASCENT
        Honest failure. The institution neither executes well nor
        has a clear reason to exist, but it is not dangerous — its
        weakness is visible.

    Q2  HIGH purpose, LOW  efficiency    DOGGEDLY SOUND
        A coherent purpose poorly executed. The book's verdict: the
        institution is salvageable and worth engaging with; if
        executed, it will move toward Q4.

    Q3  LOW purpose, HIGH efficiency     PURPOSELESS EFFICIENCY
        The book's named diseased condition. High operational
        sophistication in service of a purpose that has been
        forgotten, drifted, or was never coherent. The book's
        central diagnostic target.

    Q4  HIGH purpose, HIGH efficiency    COHERENT OPERATION
        The aim of the constructive project — including the book's
        reading of certain high-functioning firms as operating near
        this corner.

The book's political claim sits on top of this: state-sheltered
institutions drift toward Q3 because they are not subject to the
competitive test that corrects purpose-drift in voluntary markets.
Private firms can also drift toward Q3 — this is the Sears case —
when regulatory or reputational moats shield them from real
competition.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART B — WHAT THE USER DOES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

B1. Entry.

The mode opens on a QuadrantBoard with the four quadrants drawn,
labeled, and tinted. No institutions are placed yet. A
one-sentence explainer in the header reads:

    "Drop an institution into the Purpose-Efficiency Framework.
     You decide first; the book replies."

Below the board, a row of chips labeled with institutions the book
discusses (see PART D for the authored list). The user drags a
chip onto the board (or, on touch/NARROW, taps a chip and then
taps a quadrant; or, via keyboard, Tab to the chip, Enter to pick
it up, Arrow keys to move the highlight across quadrants, Enter to
drop).

Before the user drops, they are offered an OPTIONAL
DiagnosticInterview — a three-question short quiz tailored to the
chip they picked up — that helps them decide where to place it.
The interview is OFF by default to respect users who want to place
from intuition; there is a toggle "interview mode: on/off" in the
top-right of the board.

B2. The interview, when on.

For each institution chip, the fixture carries three questions,
each with 3-4 multiple-choice answers. Each answer is tagged with
a (purpose-delta, efficiency-delta) pair in {-2, -1, 0, +1, +2}.
The user's answers sum into a pair of scores, which determines the
QUADRANT SUGGESTED by the interview; the user may still place
anywhere.

Example (for: OpenAI-vs-Anthropic, an example the Guide explicitly
discusses in Ch.7):

    Q1. "What is OpenAI's stated reason for existing?"
        a. To build AGI safely for the benefit of humanity.            (+0, +0)
        b. To ship consumer AI products at scale.                      (-1, +0)
        c. To maximize shareholder/capital return.                     (-1, +0)
        d. The stated reason has shifted materially since founding.    (-2, +0)

    Q2. "How well does its current operation track whatever
         justifying argument a skeptical reader would accept?"
        a. Tightly.                                                    (+1, +0)
        b. Loosely; some activities are on-thesis, some aren't.        (+0, +0)
        c. Poorly; large parts of the operation don't fit.             (-1, +0)

    Q3. "How refined is the execution — shipping cadence,
         product polish, organizational capacity?"
        a. Cutting-edge.                                               (+0, +2)
        b. Competitive but not leading.                                (+0, +1)
        c. Mid-tier.                                                   (+0, +0)

The interview is not a quiz with right answers. The fixture's own
answer indicates the book's reading (see PART D), which is revealed
after the user places. The interview is there to slow the user
down and force specific observations rather than letting them drop
from gut feel.

B3. The reveal.

After the user drops a chip onto a quadrant, a PlacementCompare
panel opens with:

    - A small quadrant thumbnail showing the user's placement
      (outlined) alongside the book's placement (filled).
    - Agreement indicator:
          SAME QUADRANT     "You and the book agree."
          ADJACENT          "You placed one quadrant over."
          OPPOSITE          "Substantial disagreement. See below."
    - The book's reasoning: 2-3 paragraphs (serif), authored,
      drawn from fixtures. See PART D.
    - Optional: a "trajectory" indicator. Some institutions are
      discussed by the book as MOVING between quadrants over time
      (Sears was Q4 in 1970 and Q3 by 2000; the iPhone was Q4 in
      2008 and the book assesses Apple as drifting since).
      Render this as an authored arrow on the thumbnail when
      present in the fixture.
    - "OPEN IN ONTOLOGY" button: if the book's reasoning cites
      specific claims (e.g., FRAGMENTATION, SPEED-OF-CONTRADICTION),
      a button per cited claim routes back to P01 Ontology with
      that claim preselected.

C2. src/lib/prp/quadrantCases.ts

Exports:

    export type Quadrant = "Q1" | "Q2" | "Q3" | "Q4";
    export interface Placement {
      quadrant: Quadrant;
      subPosition?: { x: -1 | 0 | 1; y: -1 | 0 | 1 }; // fine-grained
      asOfYear?: number;                              // for trajectories
    }
    export interface ReasoningParagraph {
      kind: "diagnosis" | "counter-objection" | "trajectory";
      body: string;          // serif prose, author-approved
    }
    export interface InterviewQuestion {
      prompt: string;
      options: Array<{
        label: string;
        delta: { purpose: number; efficiency: number };
      }>;
    }
    export interface QuadrantCase {
      id: string;
      label: string;          // display name on chip
      kind: "firm" | "state-agency" | "category";
      bookPlacement: Placement;
      trajectory?: Placement[]; // ordered, each with asOfYear
      reasoning: ReasoningParagraph[];
      claimRefs: string[];
      interview: InterviewQuestion[];
      authorApproved: boolean;
    }

    export const QUADRANT_CASES: QuadrantCase[];

PART D — THE AUTHORED CASE LIST (v1)

Minimum authored content for shipping this mode:

  1. SEARS
     Placement (1970): Q4.
     Placement (2000-present): Q3.
     Trajectory rendered.
     Kind: firm.
     Claim refs: FRAGMENTATION, MIMICRY, SPEED-OF-CONTRADICTION.
     Reasoning must address: the shift from coherent premise (a
     general store scaled to a national network by catalog mail)
     to purposeless efficiency once the logistical premise was

  2. APPLE / iPHONE
     Placement at launch (2007-2012): Q4.
     Placement current (asOfYear: current year): dim-drift toward
     lower purpose score. Authored with care; avoid polemic. The
     book's reading is DRIFT, not collapse.
     Kind: firm.
     Claim refs: INVENTION-VS-INNOVATION, FRAGMENTATION,
     RATCHETING-STANDARD.

  3. DE BEERS
     Placement: Q3 on book's reading.
     Kind: firm.
     Claim refs: MIMICRY, PURPOSELESS-EFFICIENCY.
     Reasoning: example of a firm whose ability to sustain price
     is not traceable to coherence of the argument for diamonds-
     as-value.

  4. PALANTIR
     Placement: book's reading is near Q4 but not fully authored.
     Kind: firm.
     Claim refs: COHERENCE-SUPERIORITY, ONE-PERSON-FIRM (loosely),
     STATE-EPISTEMIC-INCOHERENCE (as the customer's incoherence,
     which is a different issue).
     Reasoning must be HEDGED: the book uses Palantir as an
     illustration of high-coherence-for-high-incoherence-customer;
     do not render as endorsement.

  5. OPENAI (SEPARATE FROM Anthropic)
     Placement: ambiguous, Q2/Q3 boundary.
     Kind: firm.
     Claim refs: SEVERANCE-OF-DIALOGUE,
     INVENTION-VS-INNOVATION.
     Reasoning: the stated purpose has visibly drifted; the
     operation is highly efficient; this is diagnostic of
     PURPOSELESS-EFFICIENCY in early form.

  6. ANTHROPIC (SEPARATE FROM OpenAI)
     Placement: Q2 leaning Q4.
     Kind: firm.
     Claim refs: COHERENCE-SUPERIORITY, FAITH-IN-REASON.
     Reasoning: the stated purpose and the operation are more
     coherent with each other than the OpenAI case; execution is
     still maturing. This is the book's reading per Ch.9.
     (NB: we are building this website on Anthropic-stack tooling;
     author-reviewed phrasing must not read as flattery. The
     reasoning paragraph should begin with a disclaimer: "Noting
     that the author has commercial ties in AI tooling, the book's
     reading here is still —" and then state the reading plainly.)

  7. A GENERIC STATE AGENCY (labeled "a state-chartered monopoly,
     e.g., a DMV or a national postal service")
     Placement: Q3 on the book's reading, by construction.
     Kind: state-agency.
     Claim refs: STATE-EPISTEMIC-INCOHERENCE, MCS.
     Reasoning must note the book's categorical commitment here:
     the state agency is NOT in Q3 because its employees are lazy
     or stupid; it is in Q3 because the coercive monopoly
     structure disincentivizes the voluntary testing through
     which purpose is maintained. This is the book's claim, and
     the reasoning paragraph should be explicit that a reader
     rejecting MCS will reject this placement.

  8. A UNIVERSITY (labeled "a regulated, accredited university")

     Kind: firm (but state-chartered; note the hybrid).

  9. AN OUTCOME-BASED TRAINING INSTITUTION (labeled "an

     Placement: Q2-to-Q4 depending on execution.
     Kind: firm.
     Claim refs: COHERENCE-SUPERIORITY,
     VALUE-AS-PERSUASION.
     Included for contrast with #8; the contrast is the

Each of 1-9 must ship with authorApproved=true. Additional cases

----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART E — COPY RULES FOR THIS MODE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

E1. Axis labels.

The X-axis tick labels are "LOW" and "HIGH". The Y-axis tick
labels are "LOW" and "HIGH". Axis names are "EFFICIENCY" and
"PURPOSE" (serif, uppercase, 12px). Do not use "good" / "bad"
labels; the book rejects the simplification (Q3 is a diseased
condition, yes, but it is not morally the author's point to
moralize institutions; the framework diagnoses).

E2. Quadrant labels.

    Q1: "STRUGGLING"
    Q2: "DOGGEDLY SOUND"
    Q3: "PURPOSELESS EFFICIENCY"  — rendered in --warn tint
    Q4: "COHERENT OPERATION"

E3. The reveal copy.

When a user places a case and the book placement is revealed, the
agreement indicator is exactly one of:

    "You and the book agree."
    "You placed one quadrant over."
    "Substantial disagreement. See below."

Nothing else. No "Great job!", no "Close!", no scores.

E4. Trajectory.

For cases with a trajectory, the reveal includes the sentence:

    "The book reads this institution as moving between quadrants
     over time. The arrow shows the book's account of its drift."

This is verbatim. Replace nothing.

E5. What the mode must not say.

  - "Correct" / "incorrect" placement (no right answer).
  - "Points" or any score.
  - "Capitalism wins" or any equivalent. The framework is not a
    vote on capitalism; see the book's own careful separation of
    market constraint from state coercion.

E6. Sensitive company names.

For #5, #6 especially, the reasoning paragraph must cite specific
observations rather than reading as corporate criticism.
Well-formed sentence template:

    "On the book's reading, [COMPANY] is placed at [Q]. The
     reasoning turns on [SPECIFIC DIMENSION]: [ONE-SENTENCE
     OBSERVATION]. This follows from [CLAIM REF], which is
     discussed in [CHAPTER]."

----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART H — IMPLEMENTER NOTES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

H1. Do not use a drag library. react-dnd and dnd-kit both exceed
    the budget (8-25KB gzip each). A hand-rolled pointer-event
    handler is ~200 lines and is strictly adequate here; the
    complexity is not in the drag mechanics but in the
    interview/compare flow.

H2. The interview is OPTIONAL. Resist the urge to make it mandatory
    "for a better experience". The mode must support both use
    patterns — gut drop and guided drop — without either being
    privileged.

H3. If a user places and then picks up the same chip again,
    treat it as a REVISION: close any open PlacementCompare,
    clear the revealed flag, and let them re-place. This is
    faithful to the Guide's own recommendation that reading
    should be iterative.

H4. Do not gate modes 3-5 on prior placement here. P02 is
    standalone-useful; P03/P04/P05 do not require any P02 state.

H5. If the implementer is tempted to add a "share this
    placement" affordance: no. This is an educational mode, not a
    social one. The panel is not a product.


End of P02.
