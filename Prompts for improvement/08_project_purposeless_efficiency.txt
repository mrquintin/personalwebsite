================================================================================
PROMPT 08 — PROJECT: PURPOSELESS EFFICIENCY (THE BOOK)
================================================================================

TASK
----
Build /purposeless-efficiency: a bespoke page for Michael's book,
"Purposeless Efficiency" — the first of a five-book series. The page is
a literary object inside the operator's workstation. It is the ONLY
surface on the site that uses a serif typeface. It is the only surface
on the site where "beauty" is allowed to take precedence over "density."

SOURCE MATERIAL
---------------
From the transcript: "I'm also writing a book, a book called — well, five
books, a five-book series. The first one I'm about to finish. The first
one is called Purposeless Efficiency. It's a big project of mine,
something that I love working on, and it talks about corporatism, it
talks about gamification of industry, incumbency, complacency, and
economic revolution."

Use those five themes (corporatism, gamification of industry, incumbency,
complacency, economic revolution) as the scaffolding of the page's
content architecture.

PHILOSOPHY
----------
Every other page on this site argues through structure. This one argues
through prose. It must feel like stepping out of a terminal into a
library. The transition is the point — the terminal is the operator's
world, and this is the operator's mind at rest. The reader arrives here
from an accordion spine that is typographically jarring on purpose.

VISUAL SPEC
-----------
Break discipline, carefully.

  - Background changes to a slightly warmer paper: oklch(0.20 0.01 75)
    instead of the site's default bg-1 — barely perceptible but
    present. Alternatively, a very faint vertical rule down the
    gutter margins suggesting a bound page.
  - Body prose: Source Serif 4 at --t-md, measured line-length 64ch,
    line-height 1.7. Hyphenation on. Optical margin alignment where
    possible (hanging punctuation).
  - Drop caps on each major section (CSS :first-letter, scaled 3x,
    floated left, aligned to cap-height).
  - All chrome (status bar, breadcrumb, page header) remains monospace.
    ONLY the body is serif. This tri-face tension is the whole point.

PAGE COMPOSITION
----------------
  1. HEADER (scaffold, UNCLASSIFIED):
       DOSSIER :: PRP · PURPOSELESS EFFICIENCY
       Subtitle in monospace --fg-dim:
         "Book I of the series · manuscript in progress"
       Breadcrumb: ~/projects/purposeless-efficiency/manuscript

  2. COVER + OVERVIEW STRIP:
       Left (40%): A monumental title block in serif:
             "Purposeless
              Efficiency"
         set at ~--t-3xl, 1.0 line-height, tight tracking, italic "i"
         in Efficiency if the face supports it. Author below in
         small caps monospace: "MICHAEL QUINTIN". Subtitle in italic
         serif at --t-md:
             "Book I · on corporatism, gamification, incumbency,
              complacency, and economic revolution"

       Right (60%): A tabular METADATA PANE (mono, reuse scaffold):
             SERIES     Five volumes
             VOLUME     I of V
             STATUS     Manuscript · MS v{n} · {n} words / {target}
             UPDATED    2026-04-12 · 8d ago
             FORMATS    digital · print (planned)
             RIGHTS     All rights reserved
             ORDER      (not yet available — join the list)

  3. PROGRESS LINE:
       A full-width 1px rule with an overlaid progress bar in monospace:
             ████████████░░░  92,143 / 100,000 words · ms v4

       The bar is made of Unicode box-drawing characters, not a DIV
       animation. Update via src/content/projects/purposeless-efficiency/
       progress.ts — a single number updated by hand.

  4. PREFACE (serif body):
       2–4 paragraphs by Michael explaining why this book exists.
       Voice: declarative, intellectual, slightly cold. Placeholder
       copy is acceptable; mark with TODO(michael). DO NOT invent
       biographical anecdotes.

  5. THE FIVE PILLARS:
       A vertical accordion (note: distinct from the landing accordion
       — this one is a classical vertical list where clicking a pillar
       expands it in place). Five items, one per theme:

         § I  Corporatism           — what it has done to work, and what
                                      it calls "ambition."
         § II Gamification          — the industry of scoring, ranking,
                                      grinding; and its psychological
                                      residue.
         § III Incumbency           — why the machine does not die when
                                      it should.
         § IV Complacency           — the citizen's withdrawal, and the
                                      elite's.
         § V  Economic Revolution   — what comes next, and how we avoid
                                      the failure modes of the last one.

       Each pillar header is in serif italic; body copy (one or two
       paragraphs expanded) is serif. Collapsed rows show the §
       numeral, the title, and a single-line seed quote in small
       italic.

  6. EXCERPT:
       A single pull-out chapter or scene — 500–1500 words — rendered
       as a reading surface (measure 64ch, drop cap, wide top margin).
       Source the content from src/content/projects/purposeless-
       efficiency/excerpt.mdx. If no excerpt is available yet, omit the
       block entirely and leave a single monospace line:
             "— excerpt coming soon —"

  7. THE FIVE-BOOK ARC:
       A horizontal strip (at desktop widths) showing the planned
       volumes:
             I. Purposeless Efficiency   [writing]
             II. {title TBD}             [planning]
             III. {title TBD}             [planning]
             IV. {title TBD}              [planning]
             V. {title TBD}               [planning]
       Each slot is a small card in monospace with a serif title.
       Titles II–V can be placeholder "TBD" — or, if Michael wants,
       authored working titles. Mark clearly as "provisional."

  8. NOTIFY LIST / RIGHTS:
       A quiet row — no splash — reading:
             "To be notified when Book I is available, write
              {email} · subject: PRP / NOTIFY. For foreign rights,
              film, or press inquiries: write the same address with
              subject PRP / RIGHTS."
       Monospace, --fg-dim, email in --fg-hi. No form. (A form would
       feel commercial; the ascetic directness is right for this page.)

  9. CITATION BLOCK (reuse scaffold patterns):
       A copy-ready citation in monospace, a BibTeX block beneath it,
       and a copy button that copies the BibTeX. Default citation:
             Quintin, M. (forthcoming). Purposeless Efficiency.
             Book I of a five-volume series.

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
-------------------
  - Serif body, monospace chrome. No sans on this page.
  - Progress bar updates correctly from a single source of truth.
  - Pillars expand and collapse with keyboard (space, enter) and mouse.
  - Excerpt respects measure (64ch) even on wide viewports.
  - Arc strip scrolls horizontally on narrow viewports.
  - Citation and BibTeX are copyable with a visible copy affordance.
  - Reading this page feels slower and more deliberate than every
    other page on the site. That is the whole test.

DO NOT
------
  - Do not add book reviews, blurbs, or "praise for" blocks until the
    book has been published and real ones exist. Inventing them is
    contemptible.
  - Do not auto-play an audiobook clip.
  - Do not add a 3D tilting book cover on hover. This is a book, not a
    gimmick.
  - Do not set drop caps on paragraphs that are too short (<4 lines) —
    they should only appear on block-opening paragraphs.

STRETCH
-------
  - A "margin notes" mode: toggleable small annotations anchored in the
    right margin, in monospace, sourced from an annotations file. Used
    in the excerpt. A delight for careful readers.
  - A word-count ticker in the status bar specifically on this page:
    "PRP · 92,143 / 100,000 (92%)". Replaces the UTC clock segment
    only while the page is mounted.
