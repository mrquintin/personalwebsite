P03 — THE DIAMOND METHOD PRACTICUM (MODE 3 of 5)
=================================================

Prerequisites: P01 (chassis), P02 (optional, but state store must
be in place).

GOAL OF THIS MODE

The Diamond Method is the book's constructive procedure for
philosophical work. It is not a trick or a heuristic; it is a
four-step discipline for turning an encountered paradox or
contradiction into a reconstructed position whose consequences
can be traced. The book is built using this method, and the
Guide presents the method in Chapter 7. A reader who understands
the method can see what the book is DOING, not only what it is
CLAIMING.

This mode teaches the method by walking the user through authored
cases in which the method is applied. The cases are not toys;
they are the cases the book itself treats — silent compulsion,
insider trading, coherence-as-truth, et al. The user makes
choices at each step, the book's treatment is revealed after the
user commits, and the user can compare, dissent, or redo.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART A — THE METHOD, AS THE GUIDE STATES IT
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The Diamond Method has four steps. They must be rendered in this
order, with these names:

    STEP 1 — IDENTIFY THE CONTRADICTION
        Locate, with specificity, an apparent contradiction: two
        claims, both held by serious thinkers or implicit in the
        structure of a position, that appear to negate each other.
        The move is DIAGNOSTIC. Success at this step is naming the
        contradiction precisely enough that it cannot be waved
        away with adjectives.

    STEP 2 — DISSOLVE OR DECLARE
        Apply the paradox/contradiction distinction (see P01
        claim PCD). Ask: is this a PARADOX (an apparent
        contradiction that resolves under clarifying
        distinctions), or is it a CONTRADICTION proper (a genuine
        incoherence that must be acknowledged)? The step produces
        one of two verdicts:
            DISSOLVED — the apparent contradiction is an artifact
                         of imprecise definitions. The step's
                         deliverable is the sharpening definition.
            DECLARED  — the contradiction is real. The step's
                         deliverable is an explicit statement of
                         which of the two positions must be
                         revised and on what grounds.

    STEP 3 — RECONSTRUCT
        Build the position that results from Step 2. If
        DISSOLVED, state the refined distinction as the new
        position. If DECLARED, state the surviving position after
        the revised side has been revised. Deliverable: a
        one-sentence position that can be tested.

    STEP 4 — TRACE CONSEQUENCES
        Follow the reconstructed position through at least three
        downstream claims. The point of the step is to distinguish
        a reconstructed position that does real work from one that
        survives only in the abstract. If the consequences are
        trivial or absent, return to Step 1 (the identified
        contradiction was not load-bearing).

The Guide emphasizes that the method is iterative: a failure at
Step 4 kicks the user back to Step 1 with what has been learned.
This iterative property must be rendered in the mode.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART B — WHAT THE USER DOES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

B1. Entry.

The mode opens with a CASE GRID: six case cards laid out in a 2x3
(WIDE), 2x3 (STANDARD), or 1-column stack (NARROW). Each card
shows:
    - Case title (serif, display).
    - The two claims in apparent tension (1 sentence each).
    - A difficulty tag: BEGINNING / INTERMEDIATE / ADVANCED.
    - A tag set showing which load-bearing claims this case
      connects to (e.g., "CRI", "MCS").

A header above the grid states:

    "Six cases the book treats. Pick one and walk the method."

No case is locked behind progression. The user may pick in any
order. The six cases are specified in PART D.

B2. The stepper.

When a user picks a case, the CASE GRID animates out (or fades,
on reducedMotion) and the DiamondStepper mounts. The stepper
shows:

    Top: a four-segment progress rail labeled 1 · 2 · 3 · 4.
    Current step is highlighted; completed steps are filled;
    upcoming steps are dim.

    Middle: the step's content. For each step, two regions:
        LEFT  — the case material at that step (e.g., Step 1
                shows the two apparently contradictory claims;
                Step 2 shows the user's PARADOX/CONTRADICTION
                choice and asks for the justification).
        RIGHT — the user input area. Inputs at each step:

            STEP 1. Three-choice "what, precisely, is the
                    contradiction?" The three options are
                    authored; one is the book's framing, two are
                    misstatements. The user picks; the choice is
                    recorded but not yet right/wrong-judged.

            STEP 2. Binary "DISSOLVE or DECLARE?" radio plus a
                    short text input for the user's justification
                    (recommended 1-3 sentences, enforced by soft
                    cap at ~300 chars). The text input is
                    recorded but never transmitted anywhere.

            STEP 3. Single-sentence input for the user's
                    reconstructed position (enforced soft cap
                    200 chars). The user may choose from three
                    authored templates OR type their own.

            STEP 4. Three short slots for consequence claims
                    (each soft-capped 200 chars). Each may be
                    left blank; if all three are left blank the
                    user cannot advance (the method's point
                    requires at least one consequence).

    Bottom: BACK / NEXT controls. BACK never loses prior input
    (the user can iterate non-destructively).

After Step 4 is submitted, the ReconstructionReveal panel opens
and shows the book's treatment alongside the user's. See B3.

B3. The reveal.

ReconstructionReveal is laid out in two columns on STANDARD/WIDE,
stacked on NARROW:

    LEFT COLUMN — YOUR WALK
        Step 1 choice, highlighted.
        Step 2 choice + justification.
        Step 3 reconstruction sentence.
        Step 4 consequence bullets.

    RIGHT COLUMN — THE BOOK'S WALK
        The same four fields, authored from the Guide.

Below the columns: a brief DIAGNOSTIC BLOCK:

    - If the user's Step 1 matches the book's Step 1 framing:
      "You named the contradiction the same way the book did."
      else "The book framed the contradiction as: [phrase]. Your
      framing: [phrase]. Differences of framing at this step
      tend to propagate; consider walking again."

    - If the user's Step 2 verdict is the same:
      "You reached the same verdict (DISSOLVE / DECLARE)."
      else "You chose [V]; the book chose [V']. This is the
      step at which the book and you most visibly part
      company."

    - Step 3 and Step 4 are not adjudicated mechanically. The
      reveal simply shows both.

Below the diagnostic block:

    ACCEPT  — user treats the book's walk as persuasive; case
              is marked "accepted" in the store.
    DISSENT — user marks the case as "dissented"; the store
              records this. A dissent is not treated as
              failure; it is a first-class outcome.
    REDO    — user walks the case again; prior walk is
              retained as a history entry viewable via a small
              "previous walks" affordance.

The store never reveals "correctness". The book has a view; the
user has a view; both are shown.

B4. Iteration.

From ReconstructionReveal, the user may:
    - RETURN to the case grid to pick another case.
    - OPEN IN ONTOLOGY any cited claim ref.
    - OPEN IN OBJECTIONS DOJO if the case is one the dojo also
      treats (see P04).

PART D — THE SIX CASES (v1)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

All six must ship with authorApproved=true. All bookWalk fields
must be authored by Michael; Guide paraphrase is acceptable as a
draft but requires author review before shipping (see P06
unpublished-work check).

CASE 1 — SILENT COMPULSION vs. VOLUNTARY MARKET
    Difficulty: INTERMEDIATE.
    Tag claims: MCS, ACT.
    Objection ref: 14.3.
    Apparent tension:
        A. Marx: market conditions compel the worker to sell
           labor; this is coercion in substance.
        B. The book: markets do not coerce; the state coerces.
    Book walk (sketch; actual authoring by Michael):
        Step 1: the contradiction is framed as "is market
                pressure coercion?" specifically, with exit as
                the hinge.
        Step 2: DISSOLVE. The sharpening distinction is between
                compulsion that permits exit (markets) and
                compulsion that prohibits exit (state). The
                distinction is categorical, not a matter of degree.
        Step 3: market pressure is real; it is not coercion.
        Step 4: consequences include (i) union activity is
                legitimate but not a response to coercion; (ii)
                state licensing regimes ARE coercive and should
                be analyzed as such; (iii) the golden-chain
                metaphor captures the phenomenology without
                miscategorizing the structure.

CASE 2 — INSIDER TRADING AS FRAUD OR AS PRICE-DISCOVERY
    Difficulty: ADVANCED.
    Tag claims: PCT, VALUE-AS-PERSUASION.
    Objection ref: (none specific; Chapter 11 discussion).
    Apparent tension:
        A. Standard view: insider trading defrauds other
           market participants by exploiting asymmetric
           information.
        B. The book: insider trading accelerates the
           incorporation of private information into prices,
           which is how the market performs its coherence-testing
           function.
    Book walk (sketch):
        Step 1: the contradiction is about the function of price.
                If price is a coherence detector, then privacy
                of information impairs detection.
        Step 2: DECLARE. Actual conflict with fairness intuitions;
                the book sides with price-function over fairness
                on epistemological grounds.
        Step 3: insider trading is a speech-act that updates the
                collective coherence assessment; it should not
                be treated as categorical fraud.
        Step 4: consequences including implications for regulation,
                for market-microstructure design, and for the
                treatment of whistleblowers.

CASE 3 — FAITH IN REASON AT THE BASE
    Difficulty: ADVANCED.
    Tag claims: CRI, FAITH-IN-REASON, ACT.
    Objection ref: 14.1.
    Apparent tension:
        A. The book's anthropology: humans are animalistic,
           irrational, violence-prone.
        B. The book's epistemology: faith in reason is the only
           posture from which engagement with reality is
           possible.
    Book walk (sketch; DISSOLVE — this is the faith-in-reason
    paradox and the Guide names it a paradox deliberately):
        Step 1: not a contradiction at the level of fact; a
                paradox at the level of stance.
        Step 2: DISSOLVE. Distinction: anthropology describes;
                epistemology prescribes. The Straussian moment
                reveals the base; faith-in-reason is the
                recommended posture despite it.
        Step 3: reasoned engagement is recommended AS A POSTURE,
                not as a description of human nature.
        Step 4: consequences about institutional design,
                argumentative combat, the limits of
                persuasion.

CASE 4 — COHERENCE AS TRUTH vs. EMPIRICAL SCIENCE
    Difficulty: ADVANCED.
    Tag claims: CRI, PCT.
    Objection ref: 14.1, 14.4.
    Apparent tension:
        A. Scientific practice: truth is tested empirically.
        B. The book: truth is coherence at the limit of inquiry.
    Book walk (DECLARE; the book sides with coherence-at-limit
    and re-inscribes empirical practice as a method for
    detecting coherence, not as a foundation).
        Author-reviewed walk required.

CASE 5 — ANARCHY WITHOUT BENEVOLENCE
    Difficulty: INTERMEDIATE.
    Tag claims: MCS, ACT.
    Objection ref: 14.2.
    Apparent tension:
        A. The argument for state abolition seems to require
           that freed people behave decently.
        B. The book elsewhere denies that people behave
           decently.
    Book walk: DISSOLVE. The state-abolition argument is
    STRUCTURAL (institutions of voluntary coordination are more
    coherent than coerced ones), not PSYCHOLOGICAL (people are
    good).

CASE 6 — INVENTION vs. INNOVATION IN A STAGNANT MARKET
    Difficulty: BEGINNING.
    Tag claims: PCT, PEF, INVENTION-VS-INNOVATION.
    Apparent tension:
        A. Inventions appear to be discontinuous, lightning-
           strike events.
        B. Innovation is incremental, driven by identifying
           contradictions in current premises.
    Book walk: DISSOLVE. The distinction is definitional;
    inventions are rarer and of a different kind from
    innovations; the book's entrepreneur is the INCREMENTAL
    INNOVATOR. Deliverable: clean definitional sharpening;
    consequences for how entrepreneurship is taught and
    rewarded.

Author is free to substitute or rename cases; the TYPES of cases
matter more than the exact labels. What must not change:
    - At least one DISSOLVE case and at least one DECLARE case.
    - At least one case from each difficulty tier.
    - At least three cases with objection refs into P04.
    - At least two cases tied to MCS or ACT (so the political
      argument is walked, not just the epistemological one).


----------------------------------------------------------------------
PART E — COPY RULES FOR THIS MODE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

E1. Step names.

Verbatim, uppercase serif, 14px:

    STEP 1. IDENTIFY THE CONTRADICTION
    STEP 2. DISSOLVE OR DECLARE
    STEP 3. RECONSTRUCT
    STEP 4. TRACE CONSEQUENCES

E2. Verdict labels.

    DISSOLVE — apparent contradiction dissolves under a
               sharpening distinction.
    DECLARE  — the contradiction is real; the book revises one
               of the two positions.

Tooltip text on the labels MUST be present for new users; the
tooltip is exactly:

    DISSOLVE: "An apparent contradiction that resolves when a
               definition is sharpened. No position is abandoned;
               a distinction is added."
    DECLARE:  "A genuine contradiction that forces revision. One
               of the two positions must be rejected, on stated
               grounds."

E3. Reveal copy.

Verbatim:

    "Your walk and the book's walk, side by side. The book's view
     is one view."

Below the columns, the diagnostic block templates (fill-ins in
brackets):

    "Step 1: [matches | differs]."
    "Step 2 verdict: [same | different (you: V, book: V')]."
    "Steps 3 and 4 are not adjudicated here."

E4. What the mode must not say.

  - "Correct" or "incorrect" at any step.
  - "You got it right."
  - "The book teaches us that..." (the book CLAIMS; it does not
    teach).
  - "Score" or any numerical competence rating.
  - "Try again" (use "walk again" or "redo").

PART H — IMPLEMENTER NOTES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

H1. The stepper is NOT a form. Do not use a form library.
    The validation rules are trivial and a small useReducer or
    zustand slice is adequate.

H2. Resist the urge to "grade" the walk. The book's posture is
    that the reader's walk and the book's walk are both views;
    the reader is equipped, by walking, to locate disagreement
    precisely. Grading destroys this.

H3. Do not render Step 1 options in a way that telegraphs which
    is the book's framing. No check-marks, no green highlight
    on the "right" answer. The user commits; the reveal shows.

H4. If Case 2 (insider trading) proves controversial in user
    testing, it may be optioned OUT of the v1 set and replaced
    with a less flammable case; the book's commitment to that
    reading is strong enough that dilution is preferable to
    a dishonest softening of the book's actual claim. That
    decision is Michael's.

H5. History of walks exists in-session; a user exiting the
    accordion and reopening in the same session should find
    their history intact. ExperienceHost's unmount/remount must
    not nuke the zustand store; see I01 for the mount contract.
