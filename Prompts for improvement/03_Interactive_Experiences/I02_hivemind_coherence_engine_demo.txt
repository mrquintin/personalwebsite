================================================================================
I02 — HIVEMIND :: "THE COHERENCE RECEIVER"
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Build the in-accordion interactive experience for the Hivemind panel.
The experience is a client-side simulation of the Coherence Engine's
five-layer scoring pipeline and the quant pre-seed VC fund's six-gate
decision policy. The visitor chooses a pitch fixture (or pastes short
text of their own), watches the engine score it layer-by-layer, then
watches the fund's decision policy convert that score plus a portfolio
snapshot into a pass / fail / manual_review decision, with a
deterministic reason code for any gate that fires.

This IS the primary body of the Hivemind panel, both on "/" (expanded
accordion panel) and on /hivemind (route). Wiring and lazy-load per I01.

THE CONCEPT IN ONE SENTENCE
---------------------------
A visitor submits a pitch and watches the exact pipeline that decides
whether Hivemind's fund would fund it — every layer scored, every gate
evaluated, every number auditable, every reason code traceable to a
documented policy.

THE INSTRUMENT
--------------
The name of this experience, displayed in the scaffold meta-strip:

    HIVEMIND :: COHERENCE RECEIVER · rcv-v1.0

"Receiver" is deliberate. It is the intake end of the fund pipeline —
the thing a pitch lands inside. It is not a "calculator" (cold), not a
"demo" (marketing), not a "playground" (dismissive). It is a receiver.

SOURCE MATERIAL
---------------
The primary source is the five Coherence Engine guides:

  Guide 1  Executive Overview              (the thesis, the fund, the
                                            five guides)
  Guide 2  The Mathematical Framework      (the five layers, the fusion,
                                            domain-relative coherence,
                                            anti-gaming, confidence)
  Guide 3  The Geometry of Contradiction   (why cosine fails, the
                                            difference-vector, the 42-
                                            of-768 dimensional
                                            concentration, the
                                            Householder reflection, the
                                            five-stage production
                                            detector)
  Guide 4  The Quant Pre-Seed VC Fund      (the six gates in order, the
                                            required-superiority core
                                            equation, the seven R-terms,
                                            the decision artifact)
  Guide 5  Architecture & Status           (package v0.1.7, waves 1–6
                                            complete, honest open
                                            problems)

The Receiver must remain faithful to these guides. When displaying
numbers, it displays the ones the guides publish; when it cannot
compute a number in the browser (e.g., an SBERT embedding of user-
typed text), it says so and uses the nearest fixture. It never invents.

SUBSTRATE (WHAT RUNS IN THE CLIENT)
-----------------------------------
All five layers and all six gates are computed in the client. The
client has access to pre-baked fixtures for:

  (a) A curated set of 8–12 pitch fixtures, each with:
        - transcript: ~400–1,200-word conversational pitch text
        - domain_primary, domain_mix
        - requested_check_usd
        - pre-computed layer traces:
            * S1 contradiction pairs (pair text, NLI label, confidence)
            * S2 argumentation graph (acceptable set, attacks)
            * S3 difference-vector projections + Hoyer sparsity
            * S4 compression ratio
            * S5 structural graph (nodes, components, cycles, depth)
        - coherence_superiority with CI95
        - anti_gaming_score
        - transcript_quality_score
        - compliance_status
        - expected decision under the default portfolio state

  (b) A portfolio-state set — a small catalog of realistic portfolio
      snapshots (nominal, stressed, defensive, over-concentrated in
      market_economics, etc.), each with the eight inputs the
      portfolio-risk R adjustment consumes.

  (c) Domain parameters (CS_d_0, γ_d, S_d_min) quoted verbatim from
      Guide 4 §3.1 for market_economics, governance, public_health.

  (d) A small bundled 3,000-sentence FAISS-proxy (title + author
      metadata) shared with I04 for the reflection demo. I02 does not
      need this, but shares the dependency if it turns out useful.

The decision-policy computation in the client is a faithful port of
the six gates in Guide 4 §4 and the required-superiority equation in
§3. It runs deterministically in single-digit milliseconds per pitch.
Verify by a round-trip assertion: for each fixture, the client's
output matches the fixture's expected_decision exactly.

USER-TYPED TEXT
---------------
A visitor may paste or type a short pitch (up to 3,000 characters) into
the Receiver. The client does NOT run SBERT or DeBERTa; instead, it
selects the nearest fixture by a cheap TF-IDF cosine against the
bundled fixture transcripts, annotates its output with "fixture-
matched: <id>" (visible in a footer), and runs the rest of the pipeline
against the matched fixture's layer traces. The visitor is told, in
plain text, what this does and why. Reproducing Guide 2 honestly
matters more than pretending to run an engine that cannot fit in the
browser.

THE INTERACTIVE STRUCTURE
-------------------------
The Receiver is a four-stage vertical flow. At any time the visitor
can scroll or jump between stages. On first mount, only stage 1 is
populated; stages 2–4 fill as the visitor advances.

  STAGE 1 — INTAKE
    Choose a pitch. Three tabs at the top of stage 1:
      [ FIXTURES ]   the 8–12 curated pitches, each with a two-line
                     gloss, a requested check size, and a domain chip
      [ PASTE ]      a textarea into which the visitor can paste their
                     own text; "fixture-match" badge appears underneath
                     as the visitor types
      [ DESIGN ]     a guided pitch builder with three sliders (domain,
                     check size, "claimed edge") that synthesises one
                     of 24 pre-authored stub pitches; useful for
                     probing the policy's shape without needing to
                     write
    Default tab: FIXTURES. Selection lands the pitch transcript in a
    right-hand preview pane with syntax-aware highlighting: one color
    for propositions the engine extracted as S1 candidates, another
    for relation phrases extracted by the parser.

  STAGE 2 — THE FIVE LAYERS
    Five layer cards render in a row (≥960px) or stack (< 960px). Each
    card is a compact, monospace panel with:

        ┌ S1 · CONTRADICTION · weight 0.30 ──────────────────┐
        │ 14 pair candidates after cosine gate               │
        │ 3 flagged contradiction · NLI confidence 0.88±0.05 │
        │ projection on ĉ: +0.21   Hoyer sparsity: 0.44      │
        │ layer score: 0.64                                  │
        │ [ inspect ]                                         │
        └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

    Inspect on any card opens a drawer on the right with the layer's
    full trace for this pitch:

      S1  the list of proposition pairs the cosine gate let through,
          each labelled with the NLI verdict and its confidence; one
          pair is shown with its difference-vector projection and the
          42-dim contradiction axis highlighted (visual: a 768-bar
          row where the active 42 bars are in --accent and the rest
          in --fg-mute at 0.2 opacity). This IS a re-use of Guide 3
          Finding 3's visual payload and is the single most memorable
          image in the Receiver — do not relegate it.
      S2  the argument graph: nodes are propositions, attack edges in
          --danger, the grounded extension highlighted. Renders as
          SVG, ≤60 nodes max for legibility. Below the graph: a
          scalar — "fraction of propositions in grounded extension".
      S3  two sparklines: the Hoyer-sparsity histogram for the pitch's
          difference vectors, and the projection-on-ĉ histogram. A
          single summary scalar: "mean projection on contradiction
          axis".
      S4  a side-by-side block: the raw transcript length, its zlib
          compressed length, and the normalised compression distance
          between the transcript and a domain-corpus fixture. A one-
          line gloss states what the number means.
      S5  a miniature force-layout of the proposition graph, with its
          connected-component count, maximum depth, and cycle count
          surfaced as numeric rows.

    At the bottom of the row of five cards: a single composite score
    with its decomposition, rendered as a horizontal stack bar where
    each segment's width is the layer's weight and each segment's
    color-intensity reflects the layer's score:

        [████████░░][██████░░░░][████████████][██████░░░░][████░░░░░░]
           S1 0.30    S2 0.20      S3 0.20      S4 0.15     S5 0.15
           0.64       0.51          0.72          0.68        0.41
        → composite coherence: 0.59
        → coherence superiority vs domain incumbent: +0.31 (CI95 0.22–0.40)

    Superiority is the figure the fund actually uses. Make it the
    largest number on this stage.

  STAGE 3 — THE SIX GATES
    A horizontal strip of six gate cards, left-to-right, in the exact
    order Guide 4 §4 requires:

        1. QUALITY  2. COMPLIANCE  3. ANTI-GAMING  4. PORTFOLIO  5. COHERENCE  6. CONFIDENCE

    The strip animates left to right: each gate evaluates in sequence,
    with a ~120ms step delay between gates (disabled under
    reduced-motion). A gate card shows:

      - its name and index
      - the specific input it consumed (e.g., quality gate shows
        transcript_quality_score = 0.91 vs threshold 0.70)
      - its verdict: PASS (--ok), MANUAL_REVIEW (--warn), FAIL
        (--danger)
      - the one-line reason code it would emit into the decision
        artifact

    ABOVE the strip: a portfolio-state selector (Nominal / Elevated /
    Stressed / Domain-overexposed / Custom). Changing the selector
    re-runs the portfolio-risk-R calculation and the coherence gate.
    The visitor sees, in real time, how portfolio conditions reshape
    the threshold. The key pedagogical moment in the Receiver is here:
    the same pitch, scored identically, passes in a nominal regime and
    fails in a stressed one, NOT because the founder got worse but
    because the fund's risk budget got smaller. Quote that sentence
    (from Guide 4 §3.2) as an attribution under the selector.

    A "peek inside R" disclosure on the right shows the seven
    r-term contributions (utilization, domain_count, pipeline,
    domain_usd, liquidity, drawdown, regime) summing to the clamped
    R in [0, 0.35]. These are pulled verbatim from Guide 4 §3.2.

  STAGE 4 — THE DECISION ARTIFACT
    The final stage renders a single monospace document with a
    pretended SHA-256 hash at the top ("computed locally; NOT the
    production hash"), followed by the JSON-shaped decision artifact
    as Guide 4 §5 specifies:

      {
        "application_id": "app_01J...",
        "policy_version": "decision-policy-v1.0.0 (browser-replica)",
        "parameter_set_id": "psv1-default",
        "domain_primary": "market_economics",
        "requested_check_usd": 250000,
        "threshold_required": 0.43,
        "threshold_decomposition": {
          "domain_base": 0.18,
          "log_scale_term": 0.25,
          "portfolio_R": 0.00
        },
        "coherence_observed_ci95_lower": 0.22,
        "decision": "pass",
        "failed_gates": []
      }

    Two affordances beneath the artifact:
      - [ copy artifact ]   copies the JSON to clipboard
      - [ replay with new seed ]   regenerates the stage-3 animation

    Post-roll strip (enforced by scaffold):
      "Coherence Engine v0.1.7 · Waves 1–6 complete · fund backend live
       in shadow. This is a client-side replica; real scoring runs
       server-side against the pinned policy version."
      [ open hivemind.ai ]   — opens in a new tab

VISUAL LANGUAGE
---------------
Dense, monospace, Bloomberg-terminal-adjacent. The Receiver is the
densest experience on the site. It is allowed to be. Some specifics:

  - Grid: 8px base. All cards align to it.
  - Palette: layer cards' top accents are in --accent; gate verdicts
    use --ok / --warn / --danger exclusively. The 42-dim axis bar is
    the single most prominent use of --accent in the page.
  - Typography: mono 13–14px for bodies, 11px for annotations. No
    serif on this experience.
  - Motion: stage 3's sequenced gate reveal is the only scripted
    motion of the experience. Everything else is instantaneous.
  - Dark-theme only (the Receiver reads wrong on a light surface; the
    scaffold's theme toggle hides itself when this experience mounts).

ADAPTATION TO hostWidthPx
-------------------------
  < 640 px    stages stack vertically; the five layer cards collapse
              into a single accordion list; the six gates render as a
              vertical column; the 42-dim bar crops to 256 dims with a
              "continues" indicator
  640–960 px  layer cards are a 3+2 grid; gates are 2 rows of 3; 42-
              dim bar renders in full but compressed
  960–1280 px row of five layer cards; horizontal gate strip; inspect
              drawer appears as a right rail
  ≥ 1280 px   everything above, plus a persistent "policy diff" side
              panel that exposes each domain's canonical parameters
              from Guide 4 §3.1

A11Y
----
  - Every layer card and every gate card is a <button> or
    <section tabindex="0"> with an aria-label that names the layer
    and its score.
  - The 42-dim contradiction axis bar is accompanied by a <details>
    that, when expanded, reads: "Contradiction signal concentrates in
    42 of 768 embedding dimensions (5.5%); this bar highlights those
    42 dimensions. The remaining 726 dimensions are shown at reduced
    opacity."
  - Stage 3's sequenced reveal honors reducedMotion: all six gates
    render their verdicts simultaneously with no step delay.
  - Each verdict change is announce()-d: "quality gate passed",
    "coherence gate failed, reason code: cs_below_threshold",
    "decision: manual review".

DATA CONTRACT
-------------
Fixtures are typed modules, not JSON. The shape, summarised:

  type LayerTrace = {
    s1: { pairs: ContradictionPair[]; score: number };
    s2: { nodes: PropNode[]; edges: ArgEdge[]; grounded: string[];
          score: number };
    s3: { sparsityHist: number[]; projHist: number[];
          meanProjection: number; activeDims: number[]; // 42 indices
          score: number };
    s4: { transcriptLen: number; compressedLen: number; ncd: number;
          score: number };
    s5: { nodes: StrucNode[]; edges: StrucEdge[]; components: number;
          maxDepth: number; cycleCount: number; score: number };
  };

  type PitchFixture = {
    id: string;            // "fx.pitch.mkt_econ.clean"
    oneLiner: string;      // for the fixture tab UI
    transcript: string;    // 400–1200 words
    domainMix: DomainMix;
    requestedCheckUsd: number;
    layerTrace: LayerTrace;
    csLower: number; csUpper: number;
    antiGaming: number;
    tqs: number;
    compliance: "clear" | "review_required" | "blocked";
    expectedDecision: "pass" | "fail" | "manual_review";
  };

  type PortfolioState = {
    id: string;
    label: string;
    utilization: number; domainCount: number; pipeline: number;
    domainUsd: number; liquidity: number; drawdown: number;
    regime: "nominal" | "elevated" | "stressed" | "defensive";
  };

Fixture catalog requirements:
  - At least one fixture per domain in {market_economics, governance,
    public_health}.
  - At least two fixtures that pass under nominal portfolio and fail
    under stressed portfolio on the coherence gate alone. This is the
    pedagogical centrepiece.
  - At least one fixture that fails on the anti-gaming gate (template
    overlap / prior-corpus echo). A visitor must be able to see the
    anti-gaming branch fire.
  - At least one fixture with a compliance_status of "review_required"
    to show the manual_review path.
  - At least one fixture where the CI95 is wide enough to trip the
    confidence gate even though coherence passes.

Portfolio catalog requirements:
  - Nominal: all r-terms at zero; R = 0.
  - Elevated: utilization trips at 0.88; R ≈ 0.01.
  - Stressed: regime stress + drawdown + liquidity all fire; R near
    the 0.35 cap.
  - Domain-overexposed: domain_count + domain_usd fire; R mid-range.

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
-------------------
  - The Receiver renders inside the Hivemind panel on "/" and on
    /hivemind at mode="route", using the shared ExperienceHost.
  - Every layer card renders a numeric score; no "—" or "TBD"
    placeholders survive.
  - The 42-dim contradiction axis bar renders 768 bars (compressed on
    narrow viewports) with exactly 42 highlighted at the active-dims
    indices.
  - Changing the portfolio-state selector visibly re-computes the
    coherence gate verdict for at least the two "boundary" fixtures.
  - Stage 3's sequenced gate reveal completes in under 1 second total
    and is replayable via the [ replay with new seed ] button.
  - The decision artifact is copyable as valid JSON and parses via
    JSON.parse() without error.
  - The 12 fixture × 4 portfolio decision-policy test passes bit-for-
    bit; the numbers in the UI match the numbers the test asserts.
  - prefers-reduced-motion collapses stage 3's reveal to a single
    instantaneous render with all verdicts shown at once.
  - No network fetches at render time.

DO NOT
------
  - Do not claim the Receiver is "the fund". It is a browser-side
    replica of the scoring and decision policy. Display the disclaimer
    in the post-roll strip.
  - Do not fabricate a "performance" number (e.g., "accuracy: 94%").
    The guides do not claim predictive accuracy; the Receiver must
    not claim it either. The Quintin Hypothesis is labelled as an
    empirical hypothesis, not a measurement, in the post-roll.
  - Do not add confetti or a "congratulations, the pitch passed"
    celebration. Passing is the expected outcome for the half of
    fixtures tuned to pass.
  - Do not re-embed user text. Not in tfjs, not in transformers.js,
    not via an API call. The nearest-fixture TF-IDF match is the only
    operation performed on user input.
  - Do not expose fund-internal trademarks or the LP deck material.
    The Receiver is public.

STRETCH
-------
  - A "shadow mode" toggle in the portfolio-state selector that runs
    the decision in shadow (Guide 4 §7), recording the result as a
    shadow_decision and showing it alongside an "enforced" decision
    for the same input. Visualises the canary calibration discipline
    without requiring any server.

  - A hand-drawn concordance between the decision artifact's JSON
    keys and the guide chapters that define them: hover a key, see a
    small popover "defined in Guide 4 §X". Dense reference-card
    behaviour that rewards careful readers.

  - A "red team" fixture — a single pitch explicitly designed to game
    the anti-gaming gate (per Guide 4 §4.3) that the gate correctly
    rejects. Display it as a fixture in its own group labelled
    ADVERSARIAL and attribute it to the red-team harness (Guide 4 §9,
    Guide 5 §9).
