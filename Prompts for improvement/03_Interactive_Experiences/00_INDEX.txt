================================================================================
00 — INDEX :: INTERACTIVE EXPERIENCES (IN-ACCORDION)
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
This prompt set defines three bespoke interactive experiences — one for
each featured project (Hivemind, Purposeless Efficiency, Theseus) —
that live INSIDE the expanded accordion panel for that project on the
landing page, and are inherited by the corresponding /<slug> route as
their primary body content.

The governing brief, verbatim from the operator:

  "Design an enjoyable and educational interactive experience for each
   project that the user can interact with to understand what the project
   is and how it works. This experience should be stored within the
   expanded accordion for the respective project, and each experience
   should be bespoke and enjoyable."

Four constraints follow from that brief and bind every prompt in this
set:

  1. BESPOKE. The three experiences must not share a control surface or
     a visual chassis. A visitor who has used one should feel they have
     encountered something different, not a themed reskin, when they
     reach the next.

  2. IN THE ACCORDION. The experience must render inside the expanded
     accordion panel on "/" AND on the full-bleed /<slug> route. It is
     the body of the panel, not a thing that opens in a modal, a
     carousel, or a new tab.

  3. EDUCATIONAL. A visitor who interacts with the experience for
     ~90 seconds must come away with a specific, falsifiable piece of
     knowledge about the project that they could not have derived from
     the synopsis alone.

  4. ENJOYABLE. Pleasure in this product comes from agency, not from
     animation. Let the visitor DO something; let the site respond
     quickly and honestly; let the result be surprising.

A NOTE ON THE COHERENCE ENGINE AS PROJECT SUBSTRATE
---------------------------------------------------
The five uploaded PDFs describe a single research-and-production
project ("The Coherence Engine"). That project has two distinct
productised applications:

  - Hivemind (the commercial instance): the Coherence Engine used as
    the scoring layer beneath a quant pre-seed VC fund. What I02
    dramatises is the engine's five-layer score and the fund's six-
    gate decision policy applied to a pitch.

  - Theseus (the research / civic instance): the Coherence Engine's
    contradiction-geometry applied to ideological corpora and live
    principle graphs. What I04 dramatises is the anti-ontology
    reflection demo ("Reverse Marxism") and the graph-level
    contradiction mapper.

Both experiences therefore use the same mathematical substrate; they
differ in what they do with it. Purposeless Efficiency shares no
substrate with either — it is a book, and its experience (I03) is
accordingly a reading surface, not an engine demo.

CONVENTIONS SHARED ACROSS I02–I04
---------------------------------
1. No live model calls in the client. Every experience runs against
   pre-computed fixtures shipped as TypeScript modules. If a layer
   requires SBERT/DeBERTa/FAISS output to be interesting, that output
   is baked in as data, labelled fixture, and exposed through a shape
   that mirrors the real engine's return type.

2. No fabricated numbers. If a panel would be hollowed-out by showing
   real-but-shipped figures (e.g., the engine's F1 = 0.777 on general-
   domain evaluation, or the 42-of-768 concentration finding), show
   those. If a panel would require numbers that do not exist yet (e.g.,
   predictive accuracy of the Quintin Hypothesis), say so in the same
   voice the research corpus uses: "empirical hypothesis, not a
   measured fact."

3. Viewport adaptation is a first-class concern. Each experience must
   render tolerably inside an expanded accordion panel (~60–75% of
   viewport width) AND on a full-bleed /<slug> route. The experience
   reads the viewport-width prop from its host; I01 specifies the
   contract.

4. Every experience has a CLEAR END. The visitor should know when they
   have seen what it does. The last visible panel of each experience
   is a "post-roll" that names the project, cites one primary
   reference, and offers one real next action (open hivemind.ai; open
   thesescodex.com; open the excerpt in full).

5. Voice. Operator voice throughout — short sentences, present tense,
   no marketing. Forbidden-word lint from Prompt 13 applies. The
   experiences are where the voice is most tested; be stingy.

6. Motion. Transitions inside an experience are ≤200ms. Nothing
   pulses or idles. prefers-reduced-motion disables every non-essential
   transition. A state change happens because the user did something.

7. Accessibility. Every interactive surface must be operable by
   keyboard alone and announce state changes through ARIA live
   regions. This is non-negotiable — the three experiences are the
   most "screen-reader-hostile"-looking parts of the site; they must
   be among the best.

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA FOR THE SET (verify after I01–I04 are all run)
------------------------------------------------------------------
  - Expanding the Hivemind panel reveals the Coherence Receiver.
  - Expanding the Purposeless Efficiency panel reveals the Galley.
  - Expanding the Theseus panel reveals the Reflection Lab.
  - Navigating to /hivemind, /purposeless-efficiency, or /theseus
    renders the SAME experience at full bleed (see I01 for the
    shared-host contract).
  - Each experience is keyboard-operable end to end, announces state
    through a polite ARIA live region, and degrades gracefully under
    prefers-reduced-motion.
  - Each experience passes the forbidden-words lint (Prompt 13) on
    every string literal and MDX snippet it ships.
  - No experience fetches anything from the network at render time.
  - A visitor who ignores every interactive control and just scrolls
    past still sees a coherent static presentation of the project —
    the interactivity is an enhancement, not a prerequisite.
