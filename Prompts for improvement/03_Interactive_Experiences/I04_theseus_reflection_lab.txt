================================================================================
I04 — THESEUS :: "THE REFLECTION LAB"
================================================================================

PURPOSE
-------
Build the in-accordion interactive experience for the Theseus panel.
The experience is a client-side demonstration of the Coherence Engine's
contradiction-geometry research (Guide 3 of the engine's five guides)
— specifically, the Reverse-Marxism / anti-ontology pipeline, applied
to a small curated corpus of ideological propositions. A visitor picks
a proposition, watches it get reflected across a chosen concept axis,
reads the nearest retrieved mirror proposition from an authored
corpus, and inspects the 42-of-768 dimensional signature that makes
the reflection a research claim rather than a party trick.

This IS the primary body of the Theseus panel on both "/" and
/theseus.

THE INSTRUMENT
--------------
The name of this experience, displayed in the scaffold meta-strip:

    THESEUS :: REFLECTION LAB · refl-v1.0

"Lab" is intentional. The Receiver (I02) is a production pipeline; the
Reflection Lab is a research instrument. It is where a visitor sees
the experimental substrate that both Hivemind's scoring layer and
Theseus's ideology monitor rely on. The Galley (I03) is a literary
surface. Each experience has its own category, by design.

RELATIONSHIP TO THE EXISTING /theseus PAGE
------------------------------------------
Prompt 09 built /theseus with three set-pieces: the principle graph,
the Noosphere console, and the first-principles list. Those surfaces
remain, but now inside the DOSSIER tab (accessible via the scaffold's
EXPERIENCE ⇄ DOSSIER toggle, per I01). The default tab is EXPERIENCE,
which is this lab.

A visitor who wants the principle graph can reach it in one click; a
visitor who wants to play with the geometry lands here first.

THE INTERACTIVE STRUCTURE
-------------------------
The Lab is a single workbench view — not a stage-by-stage flow like
the Receiver. A visitor enters a single room; everything they need is
on one surface.

  Top strip (monospace, full width):
      axis [ class | autonomy | equality | ... ]   ĉ strength · α = 2.0
      [ compute reflection ]

  Left pane (40%):  SOURCE PROPOSITION
      - A selector listing 24–40 curated propositions drawn from
        classical-liberal, Austrian, Marxist, progressive, and
        conservative sources. Each row shows a short label, the
        proposition text in serif, and the author's surname + year
        in small-caps mono beneath.
      - Above the list, a small "random" button and a "paste your
        own" tab (the Lab, like the Receiver, does a nearest-
        fixture match on pasted text and displays the matched
        fixture's pre-computed reflection).
      - Below the selected proposition: a compact stats row:
            sentence length · 23 tokens
            embedding norm · 1.000  (all-mpnet-base-v2)
            cos(source, axis_anchor_mean) · +0.18

  Centre pane (20%):  THE OPERATION
      A compact visualization of the Householder reflection:

          e_source   ──▶   v' = e_source − α · (e_source · ĉ) · ĉ

      Rendered as two small circles with an arrow between them, the
      arrow labelled with the chosen α, and the axis ĉ shown as a
      dashed line in --accent. Purely didactic; the same shape is
      drawn for every reflection.

      Beneath the diagram, a two-line cite:
        "Finding 5, Guide 3: at α = 2.0, nearest-corpus retrieval
         recovers a contradiction ≈ 84.3% of the time."

  Right pane (40%):  MIRROR PROPOSITION
      After reflection is computed:
        - The retrieved nearest proposition from the mirror corpus
          (rendered in serif), its author and year, and its source
          (also in small-caps mono).
        - A confidence strip: "recovery match: 0.84" — the fixture's
          stored probability that this retrieval is an actual
          contradiction of the source.
        - A cosine-similarity number between source and mirror
          (often surprisingly high — explain this on hover with a
          pointer to Guide 3 §9's cos ≈ 0.902 observation).

  Bottom strip (full width):  THE 42-DIM SIGNATURE
      A 768-cell bar chart, one cell per embedding dimension,
      rendering the absolute value of the difference vector
      d = mirror − source. Exactly 42 cells are highlighted in
      --accent; the remaining 726 are in --fg-mute at 0.2 opacity.
      The 42 active indices are fixture data, pre-computed.

      Above the chart: "Contradiction signal concentrates in 42 of
      768 dimensions · Finding 3, Guide 3 · 5.5% of the space".

      Below the chart: a single scalar "Hoyer sparsity of d = 0.44"
      compared against the published means:
          contradiction 0.445 · entailment 0.212 · neutral 0.087

The 42-dim signature and the Receiver's 42-dim contradiction-axis bar
are the same research payload rendered for different ends: the
Receiver uses it as a visual inside a pipeline; the Lab uses it as
the centrepiece of a geometry demo. Reuse the component if possible;
the ContradictionAxisBar from I02 should accept a config that
switches between "mask of active indices" (Receiver) and "magnitudes
of active indices" (Lab).

AXIS SELECTOR
-------------
The axis pulldown exposes a handful of pre-authored axes:

  class          anchors: class, proletariat, exploitation
  autonomy       anchors: autonomy, consent, self-determination
  equality       anchors: equality, equity, redistribution
  order          anchors: hierarchy, tradition, authority
  market         anchors: market, price, voluntary exchange

Each axis is stored as a pre-computed unit vector and a set of anchor
terms. On selection, the Lab updates the "cos(source, axis_anchor_
mean)" display in the left pane and, on "compute reflection", uses
that axis in the Householder formula.

An explanatory line under the selector (monospace, --fg-dim, one
sentence):

    "The axis is computed from the mean embedding of its anchor
     terms. Choose the axis you want a proposition reflected across."

STRENGTH SLIDER
---------------
A slider for α ∈ [1.0, 8.0], default 2.0 (the vanilla Householder
coefficient). As the visitor drags it:

  - the Householder diagram updates its α label
  - the mirror proposition does NOT change (for a given source+axis,
    only a small number of retrievals are pre-computed, typically for
    α=1.0, 2.0, 4.0, 8.0 — the slider snaps to these values)
  - the 42-dim signature updates to the stored fixture for that α

The research note associated with the slider, rendered beneath:

    "At α = 2.0, recovery is 84.3% on the project's test set; at α =
     8.0, amplified reflection reaches 86% on individual sentences
     (Guide 3, Finding 5, footnote). Strength trades retrieval
     strictness for breadth."

THE CORPUS BEHIND THE MIRROR
----------------------------
The Lab ships a ~3,000-proposition mini-corpus — a curated subset of
the sources enumerated in Guide 3 §9: Smith, Friedman, Hayek, Rand,
von Mises, Rothbard, Sowell, Thiel, Taleb (for classical-liberal /
Austrian / neoclassical), plus Marx, Engels, Luxemburg, Lenin,
Chomsky, Piketty (for progressive / Marxist), and a smaller set of
conservative and civic-republican sources. Each entry:

  - ~200–600-character proposition drawn from a public text
  - author, year, work, and a URL to the source edition
  - a pre-computed 768-dim embedding (all-mpnet-base-v2)
  - fair-use justified by the brevity and scholarly intent of
    quotation

The mini-corpus ships as a static data module. No retrievals happen
against a remote index; every reflection → retrieval pair that the
Lab can display is pre-computed at build time for every (source,
axis, α) combination.

Note on honesty: the Lab is showing retrievals that were COMPUTED IN
ADVANCE by the actual pipeline, not retrievals computed in the
browser. Show this in the Lab's footnote strip:

    "Retrievals were computed offline by the Coherence Engine
     (coherence_engine/Experiments/Reverse_Marxism/) over a ~2M-
     sentence FAISS index; the Lab displays a pre-indexed slice of
     those retrievals for 24 fixture propositions × 5 axes × 4
     reflection strengths."

THE CALIBRATION STRIP (top-right of the workbench)
--------------------------------------------------
A small persistent strip, three rows of mono:

    engine         : coherence-engine v0.1.7
    detector       : 5-stage · linear SVM · F1 0.777 (general domain)
    cross-domain   : transfer ≈ 50–65% of in-domain — Guide 3 §7

This is the Lab's honesty flag. It admits the detector's known
limitations on the same surface it showcases its successes.

"FIND THE CONTRADICTION" GAME (secondary mode)
----------------------------------------------
A secondary tab at the top of the Lab, selectable via
[ WORKBENCH | DRILL ]. Default is WORKBENCH.

  DRILL mode presents a quiz-style drill: the visitor is shown four
  propositions and asked which two are in contradiction. On submit,
  the Lab reveals the correct pair AND runs the reflection
  automatically on the chosen source, showing why the pair is
  flagged.

  Twelve drill rounds ship at launch, drawn from domains that exhibit
  the Cosine Paradox well (see Guide 3 §2): pairs with high cosine
  but opposite stance. The drill is the teaching instrument; the
  workbench is the exploration instrument. Both draw from the same
  corpus and axis library.

VISUAL LANGUAGE
---------------
Terminal-lab, glass-and-indicator. Specifics:

  - Palette: the axis ĉ is always in --accent; active dimensions are
    in --accent; contradiction-positive verdicts use --ok (a
    contradiction was confirmed at the stored threshold); inconclusive
    retrievals use --warn. Do not use --danger here; contradiction is
    not a failure state in this experience, it is the signal.
  - Typography: serif only for the proposition texts and the mirror;
    everything else is mono. The typographic switch between mono and
    serif is the signal that "this is the claim, and this is its
    mirror claim". Resist the urge to unify.
  - Grid: 8px base. The three-pane workbench uses a strict 12-column
    under the hood so the pane widths snap neatly.
  - Motion: computing a reflection runs one 200ms tween on the
    Householder diagram's arrow (the arrow grows from the source
    circle to the mirror circle). The 42-dim signature and the
    mirror text swap instantaneously.
  - Dark-theme only. A "lab" under warm paper light reads wrong.

ADAPTATION TO hostWidthPx
-------------------------
  < 640 px    The three panes stack: source → operation → mirror →
              signature. The 42-dim chart crops to 256 dims with a
              "continues" indicator.
  640–960 px  Source and mirror are two columns; operation and
              signature are full-width rows below.
  960–1280 px The full three-pane workbench as diagrammed, signature
              full-width underneath.
  ≥ 1280 px   Adds a fourth column on the right: a "principle graph
              minimap" showing where the selected proposition (and
              its mirror) land in the larger Theseus principle graph.
              Clicking the minimap opens the DOSSIER tab scrolled to
              the graph.

DATA CONTRACT
-------------
  type AxisVector = {
    id: "class" | "autonomy" | "equality" | "order" | "market";
    anchors: string[];          // ["class", "proletariat", ...]
    unit: Float32Array;         // 768-dim unit vector; fixture data
  };

  type Proposition = {
    id: string;                 // "pr.marx.capital.v1.ch7.p2"
    text: string;               // the serif text
    author: string; year: number; work: string; sourceUrl: string;
    embedding: Float32Array;    // 768-dim
  };

  type ReflectionResult = {
    sourceId: string;
    axisId: AxisVector["id"];
    alpha: 1.0 | 2.0 | 4.0 | 8.0;
    mirrorId: string;
    cosineSourceMirror: number;
    recoveryMatch: number;      // ∈ [0,1]; fixture-stored
    activeDims: number[];       // 42 indices
    dSparsity: number;          // Hoyer sparsity of the difference
    dMagnitudes: Float32Array;  // 768-dim |d|
  };

  type DrillRound = {
    id: string;
    propositions: [Proposition, Proposition, Proposition, Proposition];
    contradictionPair: [string, string];  // two proposition ids
    explanation: string;        // 80–160 words, operator voice
  };

TESTS
-----
  - Every (source, axis, α) cell referenced by the UI has a
    corresponding entry in results.ts. Unit test asserts coverage.
  - The DRILL mode's correct-pair is actually a contradiction per
    the underlying fixture; unit test asserts every round's pair is
    present in results.ts as a source/mirror match with
    recoveryMatch ≥ 0.70.
  - Playwright: axis → source → compute → mirror flow completes
    inside 1.5 seconds on a warm bundle.

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
-------------------
  - Opening the Theseus panel on "/" reveals the Reflection Lab in
    WORKBENCH mode.
  - Selecting a proposition and pressing [compute reflection] renders
    the Householder diagram, the mirror, and the 42-dim signature
    within 250ms on a warm bundle.
  - The α slider changes the displayed reflection data to the
    closest pre-computed α (snaps to 1.0, 2.0, 4.0, 8.0).
  - Switching axes re-renders the axis cosine value in the left pane.
  - DRILL mode presents twelve rounds, each fully answerable; the
    correct answer reveals the underlying reflection.
  - The 42-dim signature highlights exactly 42 bars per fixture.
  - The calibration strip always displays engine version, detector
    F1, and cross-domain caveat.
  - DOSSIER tab (the principle graph + Noosphere console from
    Prompt 09) still renders and is reachable in one click.

DO NOT
------
  - Do not present the reflection as a claim about the truth of
    either proposition. The mirror is the corpus's nearest
    contradiction, not a refutation. Make this explicit in the post-
    roll strip.
  - Do not invent quotations or attribute text to authors incorrectly.
    Every proposition in the corpus must be a real published sentence
    with a verifiable source URL. The Lab's honesty is load-bearing.
  - Do not render the anti-ontology as a "winner". No celebratory
    language, no "beat the opponent" framing. This is a geometry
    demo, not a debate tool.
  - Do not claim the reflection is "automated rebuttal". It isn't.
    The decoder problem (Guide 3 §11) forbids that claim; corpus
    retrieval is not generation.
  - Do not use --danger for contradiction results. Contradiction is
    the signal, not the error.
  - Do not fetch the corpus from a remote source at runtime. Ship it.

STRETCH
-------
  - A "build your own axis" affordance: the visitor types 2–5 anchor
    terms; the Lab linearly combines their pre-computed embeddings
    (the terms MUST be in a small bundled dictionary of ~2,000
    precomputed anchor embeddings) and normalises. If any typed term
    is not in the dictionary, the Lab rejects the axis and surfaces
    "term not indexed" with the in-dictionary alternatives.

  - A "compare two axes" mode: render two stacked workbenches for
    the same source, one per axis, with the two resulting 42-dim
    signatures overlaid in the bottom strip. Teaches that the 42
    active dimensions differ per axis.

  - An "ontology mapper" mode (Guide 3 §8): load a small ontology
    (5–10 chapters, 30–60 propositions) and display the
    graph-propagated contradiction edges it produces. This is the
    bridge from the Lab to the principle graph on the DOSSIER tab;
    done well, it is the single most persuasive surface on the
    Theseus page.
