§B ACCEPTANCE
-------------
B1.  voice-guide.md sections:
       ## What v2 sounds like
         3 paragraphs describing the v2 register: declarative,
         calibrated, sometimes wry, never grandstanding. The
         contrast with v1 (which leaned into terminology and
         atmosphere) is named explicitly.

       ## Diction principles
         - Concrete over abstract.
         - One claim per sentence.
         - Avoid jargon unless defined in the same paragraph.
         - First person ("I think", "I'm working on") over passive
           voice or institutional plurals.
         - Numbers when meaningful; otherwise plain language.

       ## Sentence-length rhythm
         Vary. The guide gives one example paragraph that
         demonstrates a long-medium-short cadence.

       ## What to avoid
         - "synergy", "ecosystem" (corporate-speak)
         - "literally" as intensifier
         - "leverage" as verb
         - exclamation marks
         - emojis in body copy
         - hedges that erase the claim ("perhaps maybe possibly")
         - rhetorical questions in headers

       ## Voice samples
         3-5 short paragraphs (400-800 words total) drawn from
         existing repo content (essays, biography, project
         descriptions) that exemplify the v2 voice. These are also
         the seed for src/content/llm/voiceSamples.ts (suite 18/P08).

       ## When the LLM-of-me speaks
         Defines that the LLM uses this same voice. Explicitly notes
         when the LLM should fall back to plain explanation rather
         than imitating the register (e.g., when speaking to a
         clearly novice visitor).

B2.  src/content/forbidden-words.ts updated to enforce the "what to
     avoid" list. A simple build-time linter (already exists or
     needs adding via prose-lint script — confirm before adding)
     scans content/* for matches. Per-occurrence override allowed
     via /* voice-allow */ comment in the source.

B3.  Tone calibration: every section heading + every example in the
     guide is itself written in v2 voice. The guide IS its own demo.
