P05 — MODE 4, THE REFUSAL PANEL
=================================

ROLE: You are implementing MODE 4 of the Theseus Deep experience.
The Refusal Panel is the mode that answers the question: "what
happens when a publication candidate fails rigor?" The
answer it must demonstrate, interactively, is refusal — not as
an error state, not as a 400-variant pass, but as a first-class
output of the firm's machinery. The mode renders the rigor
gate's nine declared checks as an animated sequence, surfaces
the six-layer aggregator's UNRESOLVED output as its own verdict
on one of the four authored PublicationCandidates, and exposes
the founder-override path — permanent, public, ledger-recorded
— as the system's one escape hatch from the gate.

This mode is the most rhetorically load-bearing of the five.
The entire Theseus firm rests on the claim that refusal is a
signal rather than a failure, and a visitor who leaves the
Theseus panel believing anything else has left with a
misapprehension. The mode is arranged to prevent that
misapprehension by literalizing it: REFUSE and UNRESOLVED are
rendered at the same visual and semantic level of the UI as
PASS, and the sidebar's overrides-ledger makes the cost of
bypassing refusal visible.

Read P01, P02, P03, and P04 first. This prompt
assumes the chassis, OrdersSlice, ReflectionSlice, and
SeveritySlice are in place and that sources.ts already carries
the keys enumerated in P01–P04.

GROUNDING

All content in this mode is grounded in:

  TH1 §5     — "What Theseus is not"; the anchor that the rigor
               gate runs nine checks, founder overrides are
               public and ledger-recorded, and the 100%-pass
               rate inference.
  TH4 §2.3   — the six-layer coherence subpackage (nli,
               argumentation, probabilistic, geometry,
               information, judge) and the aggregator's
               deliberately-conservative UNRESOLVED output.
  TH5 §3     — the six-layer coherence stack rendered in its
               pipeline role.
  TH5 §8     — the adversarial challenge subsystem (four
               states: pending, addressed, fallen, fatal) —
               fallen unaddressed challenges block the
               adversarial-status check.
  TH5 §10    — the peer-review swarm (seven reviewer roles) —
               feeds the peer-review-completeness check.
  TH5 §11    — decay policies (fixed-interval, evidence-
               changed, method-version-bump, embedding-drift,
               outcome-observed, calibration-regression) —
               feeds the freshness check.
  TH5 §12    — the rigor gate itself: the nine declared
               checks, verdict states, override discipline,
               and the 100%-pass-rate inference.

The canonical nine-check list, taken verbatim from TH5 §12
and stored byte-identically in sources.ts under the key
TH5_SEC_12_NINE_CHECKS:

    "Nine declared checks run in parallel. Representative
    checks:
    • Provenance completeness — the cascade path from
      publication conclusion back to source artifacts is
      unbroken.
    • Calibrated confidence — the conclusion's confidence is
      within calibration-appropriate bounds for the
      producing method.
    • Peer review completeness — all major and blocker
      findings have been rebutted.
    • Freshness — no supporting claim is stale or retired at
      submission time.
    • Adversarial status — no unaddressed fallen challenges
      exist for this conclusion.
    • Cascade soundness — no cycles in the evidence graph;
      single-point-of-failure nodes are disclosed if present.
    • Method signatures — every method in the chain is
      registered, versioned, and its code has a valid
      signature against the registry.
    • Publication conditions — if present, are machine-
      readable and loaded into the public site's metadata
      for the conclusion.
    • Corpus hash — the hash of the corpus state at
      publication is recorded and pinned to the publication
      entry."
                                                    (TH5 §12)

The 100%-pass-rate inference, rendered verbatim in the
persistent footer, stored under
TH5_SEC_12_HUNDRED_PERCENT_ANCHOR:

    "The monthly refusal rate is published on /methodology/
    rigor/. A 100% pass rate for multiple consecutive months
    would be evidence that the gate is too weak, not that
    the firm is flawless."
                                                    (TH5 §12)

The UNRESOLVED discipline, verbatim, stored under
TH4_SEC_2_3_UNRESOLVED_DISCIPLINE:

    "The aggregator is deliberately conservative: when
    layers disagree past a threshold, the output is
    unresolved rather than a point verdict. unresolved is a
    first-class output in every downstream consumer, not an
    error state."
                                                    (TH4 §2.3)

The six-layer names are TH4 §2.3's exact labels:

    nli.py              — NLI (entail / neutral / contradict)
    argumentation.py    — Dung-style admissibility
    probabilistic.py    — joint satisfiability
    geometry.py         — embedding-geometry test
    information.py      — compressibility / entropy
    judge.py            — LLM-as-judge

These names are shown in the SixLayerAggregator sub-panel
(§D item F). They are NOT conflated with any CE-suite
vocabulary; cross-suite isolation is enforced by the
banned-token list in P07's prose lint.

overridesLedger is APPEND-ONLY. No action in the slice removes
or rewrites entries. Changing modes or clearing other slices
preserves the ledger in full. This is the intentional mirror
of the production architecture's "Founder overrides are public
and ledger-recorded; every override lives on the public
site's /methodology/overrides/ page with full justification,
forever" (TH5 §12). The comment block at the top of the slice
definition MUST cite this passage.

Cross-slice coupling:
  - confirmOverride's behavior depends on the active
    candidate's authored gateVerdict — read from
    selectActiveCandidate — not from any transient UI state.
  - When pickCandidate is called with a candidate whose id
    references a Severity fixture (see F3 below), no state is
    mutated in SeveritySlice. The cross-mode pointer is a
    render-only nav affordance in the NineCheckPipeline row
    for the adversarial_status check.

State selectors:

    selectActiveCandidate(state): PublicationCandidate | null
    selectPipelineProgress(candidateId):
      { cursor: number; totalChecks: 9;
        complete: boolean; verdicts: CheckOutcome[] }
    selectOverrideCount(candidateId): number
    selectOverrideCountTotal(): number

    export function assertPublicationCandidate(
      f: unknown
    ): asserts f is PublicationCandidate {
      invariant(typeof f === "object" && f !== null, ...);
      const p = f as Record<string, unknown>;
      invariant(typeof p.id === "string" && p.id.length > 0,
        "id required");
      invariant(typeof p.title === "string"
        && (p.title as string).length <= 80,
        "title ≤ 80 chars");
      invariant(typeof p.oneLineSummary === "string"
        && (p.oneLineSummary as string).length <= 180,
        "oneLineSummary ≤ 180 chars");
      invariant(Array.isArray(p.checkOutcomes)
        && (p.checkOutcomes as unknown[]).length === 9,
        "checkOutcomes must have exactly 9 entries " +
        "(TH5 §12: 'Nine declared checks run in " +
        "parallel.')");
      // The 9 CheckIds must be present exactly once each:
      const seenIds = new Set<string>();
      for (const o of p.checkOutcomes as unknown[]) {
        assertCheckOutcome(o);
        seenIds.add((o as CheckOutcome).id);
      }
      invariant(seenIds.size === 9,
        "checkOutcomes must cover all 9 CheckIds exactly " +
        "once");
      invariant(
        ["PASS","PASS_WITH_CONDITIONS","REFUSE"]
          .includes(p.gateVerdict as string),
        "gateVerdict must be one of the 3 literals");
      invariant(typeof p.gateVerdictRationale === "string"
        && (p.gateVerdictRationale as string).length > 0,
        "gateVerdictRationale must be non-empty");
      const s = p.sixLayerAggregator as Record<string, unknown>;
      invariant(typeof s === "object" && s !== null,
        "sixLayerAggregator required");
      invariant(Array.isArray(s.layerOutcomes)
        && (s.layerOutcomes as unknown[]).length === 6,
        "Exactly 6 layerOutcomes (TH4 §2.3: 'the six-layer " +
        "coherence stack').");
      const layerIds = new Set<string>(
        (s.layerOutcomes as any[]).map(x => x.layerId)
      );
      invariant(
        ["nli","argumentation","probabilistic","geometry",
         "information","judge"]
         .every(id => layerIds.has(id)),
        "All six layer ids must appear exactly once.");
      invariant(
        ["coherent","incoherent","unresolved"]
          .includes(s.aggregatorVerdict as string),
        "aggregatorVerdict must be one of the 3 literals");
    }

    export function assertPublicationCandidateArray(
      arr: unknown
    ): asserts arr is readonly PublicationCandidate[] {
      invariant(Array.isArray(arr),
        "PublicationCandidate array must be an array");
      invariant(arr.length === 4,
        "MODE 4 spec requires exactly 4 " +
        "PublicationCandidate fixtures (F1 clean, F2 " +
        "conditions, F3 refuse-adversarial, F4 refuse-" +
        "unresolved).");
      const verdicts = arr.map((f: any) => f.gateVerdict);
      invariant(verdicts.filter(v => v === "PASS").length
        === 1, "Expected exactly 1 PASS fixture");
      invariant(verdicts.filter(
        v => v === "PASS_WITH_CONDITIONS").length === 1,
        "Expected exactly 1 PASS_WITH_CONDITIONS fixture");
      invariant(verdicts.filter(v => v === "REFUSE").length
        === 2, "Expected exactly 2 REFUSE fixtures");
      const aggregatorVerdicts = arr.map(
        (f: any) => f.sixLayerAggregator.aggregatorVerdict
      );
      invariant(aggregatorVerdicts.filter(
        v => v === "unresolved").length === 1,
        "Exactly one fixture must carry aggregator " +
        "UNRESOLVED (MODE 4 F4 requirement; P07 " +
        "UNRESOLVED-fidelity snapshot test).");
      for (const f of arr) assertPublicationCandidate(f);
    }

Both guards run in the module initializer of
publicationCandidates.ts. In development, failing any
invariant throws with the full message. In production, the
array-level guard throws once at first import and the panel
renders the authored error state (an outer error boundary is
assumed from I01).

belongs to the Coherence Engine project in the HVM panel.

mention of the five-layer vocabulary in any Theseus
fixture string.

Efficiency strategic face) vocabulary appears here: no
Nash-GT, Porter-5F, Christensen-DI, Drucker-MBO, or
Kahneman-BE references.

P07's prose lint mechanically enforces these exclusions
via its banned-token list.
