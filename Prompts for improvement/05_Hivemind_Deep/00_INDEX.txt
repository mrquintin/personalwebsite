----------------------------------------------------------------------
WHY THIS SUITE EXISTS (the design thesis)
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The Hivemind accordion panel must teach the user, in three to ten
minutes of voluntary interaction, the answer to four specific
questions:

  1. WHAT IS HIVEMIND? An adversarial multi-agent strategic AI,
     not a single LLM call, not a chatbot, not a consulting-firm-
     with-AI-bolted-on.

  2. HOW DOES IT DELIBERATE? Theory network proposes -> critiques
     each other -> revises; Monitor aggregates similar solutions
     into clusters; Practicality network scores and can veto the
     whole set; full audit trail is retained.

  3. WHAT CAN THE CLIENT CONTROL? Three integer knobs:
     sufficiency (how many unique clusters before halt), feasibility
     (veto threshold), density (per-agent KB token count, which
     implicitly sets the number of theory agents).

  4. WHY IS THE FIRM BEHIND IT SHAPED THE WAY IT IS? Because it
     is engineered to neutralize four specific structural failures
     of the consulting industry, and each team and hiring procedure
     at The Nash Lab exists to dissolve one of those failures.

These four questions are the teaching goals. The five modes
below map onto them.

Standing constraints:

  (a) No runtime LLM calls. Every "deliberation" shown inside the
      experience is a replay from authored fixtures. Live LLM
      calls would (i) break the 80KB bundle contract, (ii) expose
      API keys, (iii) make the experience flaky, and (iv) conflate
      marketing demo with actual product. Replay is honest; live
      is not, because the client-infrastructure-deployment is a
      core part of Hivemind's honesty pitch.

  (b) No marketing language. The Nash Lab's public posture is
      explicit: "rigor, without the ivory tower" and "we disclaim
      responsibility for conclusions drawn by the client". The
      experience must not soften that into "try our AI today".

  (c) Client-infrastructure deployment must be legible. The
      accordion panel itself is web-served, so it cannot literally
      demonstrate on-prem install. It must, however, make the
      architectural claim visible — e.g., the ownership of the
      audit trail, the one-way firm->client updates, and the
      explicit disclaimer all surface in authored microcopy that
      can be reviewed.

  (d) Panel must be legible at hostWidthPx down to 360. Same
      viewport thresholds as I01 and the 04 suite.


----------------------------------------------------------------------
THE FIVE MODES
----------------------------------------------------------------------

Mapped to the four teaching questions (question 1 is the whole
panel, so each mode contributes to it):

  MODE 1 — DELIBERATION THEATER             (P01)
      Answers Q2 primarily, Q3 secondarily. A step-through
      replay of one full Hivemind run on an authored strategic
      problem. Theory network generates -> critiques -> revises;
      Monitor aggregates; Practicality scores; veto-or-pass;
      audit emitted. Playable, scrubbable, pauseable, keyboard-
      navigable. The single most important mode in the suite.

  MODE 2 — KNOB BENCH                       (P02)
      Answers Q3 primarily. The three client-set integers
      (sufficiency, feasibility, density) are isolated on a
      simplified fixture so the user can see how each knob
      changes the shape of the output — round counts, cluster
      counts, veto frequency — without running a full theatrical
      replay each time. Precomputed fixture matrix keyed by
      (suff, feas, dens) tuples.

  MODE 3 — FOUR FAILURES MAP                (P03)
      Answers Q4 primarily. The consulting industry's four
      structural failures (hiring, analysis, accountability,
      theatrics) paired with the four structural responses
      (blind-exam hiring, peer-reviewed KB + adversarial theory
      network, client-infra deployment + audit trail, direct
      output without slide-deck theater). Each failure expands
      into specifics drawn from Guide I.

  MODE 4 — KNOWLEDGE PIPELINE               (P04)
      Answers Q1 secondarily, Q2 secondarily, and exposes the
      substrate of both. A stepped animated pipeline showing
      how a peer-reviewed document becomes a retrievable slice
      becomes a theory agent: upload -> LLM optimize -> chunk
      -> embed (MiniLM, 384-dim) -> upsert (Qdrant, cosine)
      -> retrieve (top K, filtered by document_type). The
      pipeline is grounded in Guide III Section 2.

  MODE 5 — AUDIT ARTIFACT                   (P05)
      Answers Q1 structurally (the audit trail is the key
      fiduciary artifact). A scrubbable timeline of a complete
      deliberation's transcript: every utterance, critique,
      revision, score. Export button shows the file format and
      its ownership (client, not firm).

P06 delivers copy rules, fixture integrity, unpublished-work
posture, a11y budgets, and the ship-gate verification protocol,
mirroring 04/P06.

RELATIONSHIP TO I02
----------------------------------------------------------------------

I02 was built around the Coherence Engine's decision-policy
vocabulary: five scoring layers, six gates, 42-dim contradiction
axis, portfolio-state selector. None of that vocabulary appears in
the three Hivemind guides. The correct reading is:

  - Hivemind (per the three guides) is an adversarial multi-agent
    strategic AI. Its components are the theory network, the Monitor,
    the practicality network, the sufficiency/feasibility knobs,
    and the audit trail.

  - The Coherence Engine, IF it is still an active workstream, is
    something else — either (a) an internal decision-policy design
    for an investment fund at The Nash Lab, not part of the Hivemind
    product, or (b) a superseded earlier framing of a subset of the
    Hivemind substrate that has been replaced by the product spec
    in the three guides.

    Until Michael tells me which, the suite does NOT merge the two.
    I02 was retired 2026-04-21 and its file is now a tombstone
    (see 03_Interactive_Experiences/I02_hivemind_coherence_engine_demo.txt);
    the retired spec content is available in git history prior to
    the tombstone commit if it needs to be recovered. The Hivemind
    panel experience is the one in this suite, and the technical
    face (Coherence Engine) ships from the 06 suite under the same
    HVM panel per I01's projectId convention.

If the Coherence Engine remains load-bearing and needs its own
surface, three paths are available:

    Option A. A separate workstream outside HVM — a new project
    panel, or a fund-specific microsite, with its own experience
    suite modeled after the 04/05 pattern.

    Option B. A sixth mode in this suite's chassis —
    "CONTRADICTION GEOMETRY" — that reuses I02 design work.
    Added as P07 if desired.

    Option C. A mode INSIDE the Four Failures Map or the Knowledge
    Pipeline, framed as one specific theory-agent package
    ("coherence-engine fund-decision agent") available in the
    peer-reviewed KB. Most conservative integration.

WHAT THIS SUITE IS NOT
----------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Not a sales demo. No "start free trial", no "request a demo",
    no email capture. The words "demo" and "trial" are both
    banned in authored copy.

  - Not a chatbot playground. No text input that goes to a live
    LLM. All input is control-surface interaction with precomputed
    fixtures.

  - Not a consulting-firm website. The HVM panel represents the
    PRODUCT, with the firm (The Nash Lab) presented as context
    inside the panel — specifically inside the Four Failures Map
    mode and the CORE BELIEFS drawer it opens.

  - Not an architecture brochure. The Knowledge Pipeline mode
    teaches the RAG substrate, but it is faithful to Guide III's
    concrete choices (MiniLM, Qdrant, cosine, 384-dim, 800-token
    chunks with 80-token overlap) and does not pretend these are
    "vendor-agnostic design decisions". They are the choices.

  - Not an LLM. All copy is authored, lives in fixtures, and
    passes the prose lint in P06.

ACCEPTANCE FOR THE FULL SUITE
----------------------------------------------------------------------

The HVM experience ships when:

  [ ] All five modes mount inside the HVM accordion at host widths
      >=360px without clipping or horizontal scroll.
  [ ] EXPERIENCE <-> DOSSIER toggle from I01 preserves existing
      dossier content unchanged.
  [ ] No runtime LLM or network calls. No secrets in bundle.
  [ ] Bundle size: total HVM experience <=80KB gzip.
  [ ] Authored prose passes the P06 prose lint.
  [ ] Keyboard: every interactive element reachable; ESC closes
      drawers; playback controls have keyboard equivalents.
  [ ] reducedMotion: no translations, scales, or parallax;
      opacity-only transitions.
  [ ] aria-live announcements on mode change, playback events,
      knob changes, failure expand, pipeline stage, audit event.
  [ ] The disclaimer "The Nash Lab disclaims responsibility for
      conclusions drawn by the client" appears verbatim as the
      first sentence of a three-sentence disclaimer block (the
      second and third sentences being "The audit trail is owned
      by the client. The firm does not read it.", taken from
      Guide II §8). The full three-sentence block renders in
      exactly ONE user-reachable surface in the suite — the P05
      Audit Artifact Export Preview. The first sentence is the
      integrity-check anchor; P06's assertAudit searches for it
      by itself (not the full three-sentence block) and must
      find exactly one match across all fixture strings.
