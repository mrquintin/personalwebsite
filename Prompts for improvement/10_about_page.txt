================================================================================
PROMPT 10 — /about — THE ABOUT PAGE
================================================================================

TASK
----
Build /about: a bespoke page about Michael Quintin. This is the site's
biographical surface. It reuses the shell and scaffold, but its body is
custom. The accordion's panel 01 (ABT) teases it; this is the full page.

PHILOSOPHY
----------
Resist the portfolio About-page instinct. Do not open with "Hi, I'm
Michael." Do not list hobbies. Do not use "passionate about." An About
page on this site is a small curated dossier on a person — not a
social profile. Aim for the register of an archivist's bio card, or a
late-Updike author note, or the "principal investigator" block on a
research lab's homepage.

PAGE COMPOSITION
----------------
  1. HEADER (scaffold, UNCLASSIFIED):
       DOSSIER :: ABT · MICHAEL QUINTIN
       Subtitle: a single declarative sentence summarizing him
                 professionally. TODO(michael) — placeholder:
                 "Operator, writer, and founder of Hivemind."
       Breadcrumb: ~/about/README

  2. IDENTITY BLOCK (monospace key/value, full width):
       NAME          Michael Quintin
       ROLE(S)       Founder of Hivemind · Author · Researcher
       BASED         (city) (TODO — provide if public; otherwise
                     "North America")
       CHANNELS      email · LinkedIn · GitHub · X (as applicable)
       PGP (opt.)    fingerprint (if Michael publishes a key)

  3. BIOGRAPHY (sans-serif long-form):
       3–5 paragraphs, --t-md, 72ch measure, Inter. Third-person voice
       is acceptable and arguably better for this aesthetic; first-
       person is also fine if Michael prefers. Topics to cover — but
       DO NOT INVENT specifics:
         - What Michael works on and why.
         - The throughline between Hivemind (strategic analytical
           software), Purposeless Efficiency (book on corporatism,
           gamification, incumbency, complacency, economic
           revolution), and Theseus (ideological contradiction
           monitoring). There is a throughline — the operator
           trying to think clearly in a world optimized against
           clear thinking — and naming it well is the job of this
           section.
         - Intellectual genealogy: who he has read, what fields he
           draws from. (Placeholder; mark TODO.)
         - Current preoccupations.
       Do NOT include cute facts ("I love coffee / hiking / my dog").
       Do NOT include a "fun fact."

  4. BELIEFS / WORKING STYLE (sidebar or inline list):
       A tight monospace list — 5 to 9 items — articulating how
       Michael works or what he believes about work. This is the
       piece that makes an About page memorable. Each item is one
       sentence, declarative, with a numeric prefix:
         01 · Accuracy over warmth. Rigor over pace.
         02 · Density over decoration.
         03 · Build the interface first; let the content earn its way in.
         04 · First principles before citations.
         05 · ...
         (TODO(michael) — Michael should author these. Provide plausible
          placeholders and mark them for review.)

  5. WORK LEDGER (compact, reuses projects loader):
       A short, auto-populated list of active and recent projects,
       drawing from src/content/projects/. Three columns per row:
         code · title · one-line status
       Links to the project page. Excludes "archived" unless a query
       string says ?show=all.

  6. WRITING (optional, inline block):
       If/when a /writing or /blog section exists, pull the last 3
       entries here. For now, leave a stub:
         "writing ........................ coming soon"
       Rendered in monospace, --fg-dim. No images.

  7. CONTACT STRIP (footer of page):
       One line, monospace:
         "Direct: michael@{domain} · LinkedIn: /in/michaelquintin ·
          GitHub: /mrquintin"
       The email is the primary channel. This is not a form. Forms
       for contacting a person you want to respect are impersonal.
       (If Michael wants a form, defer to Prompt 15 where form
       backends are discussed.)

  8. COLOPHON (small, bottom):
       A quiet block listing the site's construction:
         "Built with Next.js, TypeScript, and restraint. Typography:
          JetBrains Mono, Inter, Source Serif 4. Designed around
          a deliberate absence of decoration."
       Rendered in --t-xs, --fg-mute. Replace "JetBrains Mono" if
       a different mono was shipped.

VISUAL NOTES
------------
  - Primary body face: sans-serif (Inter). Chrome and list items
    stay monospace.
  - Do not include a photograph unless Michael provides one. If he
    does, render it at 160×160, 1px border, monospace caption below
    ("MQ · 2026"). No rounded corners.
  - Decorative typographic ligatures (fi, fl, ff) should be enabled;
    this is the one page where the extra craft shows up.

DATA CONTRACT
-------------
  src/content/about/
    ├─ identity.ts          { name, roles[], based, channels[] }
    ├─ biography.mdx
    ├─ beliefs.ts           [{ n, text }]
    └─ colophon.ts          { tech[], type[], tagline }

FILES TO PRODUCE
----------------
  src/app/about/page.tsx
  src/components/about/IdentityBlock.tsx
  src/components/about/BeliefsList.tsx
  src/components/about/WorkLedger.tsx     // re-uses projects loader
  src/components/about/Colophon.tsx

ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
-------------------
  - The page renders end-to-end with placeholder content clearly
    marked TODO(michael).
  - No photograph is hardcoded; the component gracefully renders
    without one.
  - The beliefs list is typographically tight; each item fits on one
    line at desktop width without wrapping.
  - The work ledger auto-updates when a new project is added under
    src/content/projects/.
  - The contact strip exposes a valid mailto: link and the correct
    LinkedIn / GitHub handles.

DO NOT
------
  - Do not include a "download my CV" button here. CV access is the
    job of /resume (Prompt 11) and the command palette.
  - Do not include testimonial quotes.
  - Do not list skills as a tag cloud. Skills are demonstrated by the
    work ledger; tags are a cope.
  - Do not include a timeline of life events. Biographies on this
    site are thematic, not chronological.

STRETCH
-------
  - Reading preferences: a small section listing 5–10 books that
    have shaped Michael's thinking. Format: one line each, monospace,
    author and title, no cover images, no Amazon affiliate links.
  - A "now" block (after the derekSivers /now convention): one short
    paragraph updated monthly describing Michael's current focus.
    Stored in src/content/about/now.mdx; a git hook could surface its
    last-modified date as a "now — 2026-04-12" stamp.
